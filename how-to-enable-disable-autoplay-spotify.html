How to Enable or Disable Autoplay on Spotify

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, May 4, 2024

No two Spotify users are alike. Some people take their music lineup very seriously. And once they've chosen what to listen to, that flow can't be interrupted. But if autoplay is turned on, Spotify can, unfortunately, ruin your playlist by adding different suggestions at the end.

Fortunately, it's easy to manage Spotify to suit your preferences. Stick around to learn how to enable or disable autoplay on Spotify, depending on what you like.

What Is Autoplay on Spotify?

Have you ever played a playlist on Spotify, and once it was over, the app randomly started playing something else? It can be weird if you aren't used to it, but that's autoplay.

Spotify's autoplay feature keeps the mood going by playing music once you've reached the end of your playlist, album, or song selection. The trick is that it plays songs similar to what you were listening to. This ensures you don't get thrown off and can also help you discover new songs.

For instance, if you were streaming a rap playlist, autoplay can start playing songs from that same genre. You can also add the songs to your Spotify library if they don't quite fit the playlist you were streaming. It ensures that the party never stops.

So, if you enjoy always having music playing in the background, this feature is for you. It's helpful when you're too busy to manage what you're listening to.

How to Enable or Disable Autoplay on Spotify (Mobile)

Some people find value in using Spotify's autoplay feature. Others can't stand to have the app play something on their behalf. No matter which category you fall in, you can enable or disable autoplay almost instantly. The process is pretty straightforward; all you need to do is follow these steps:

  • Open the Spotify app on your phone.
  • Tap the Settings icon in the top-right corner and choose the Playback option.
  • Toggle on Autoplay similar content.
    • Close

    That's it; Spotify will now ensure you always have something to listen to when your music runs out. You can disable autoplay by following the same steps and toggling the setting off.

    How to Enable or Disable Autoplay on Spotify (Desktop)

    We know you sometimes use Spotify on your computer, be it at work or home. Fortunately, managing Spotify's autoplay feature is quick and easy to do on the desktop app as it is on the mobile app. Here's how:

  • Open the Spotify app on your computer.
  • Click the Profile icon in the top-right corner of the screen and select Settings.
  • Look for the Autoplay option and toggle it on (click on the toggle to turn it green).

    • To disable autoplay, follow the same steps and toggle Autoplay off.

    Another interesting method to discover new music is using Spotify's AI DJ. You read that right; Spotify introduced the AI DJ in February 2023. It's an interesting feature that plays songs you might enjoy and even includes commentary by a human-like voice. If that's up your alley and the feature is available in your country, consider trying it out to see how well Spotify knows you.

    Take Control of Your Spotify Experience

    When it comes to streaming platforms like Spotify, the power is in your hands. You can get the experience you prefer if you know what settings to tweak in the relevant app. As with other settings, learning how to use autoplay is a must-have tip of you're a hardcore Spotify user.

    Spotify's autoplay feature is handy for those who love to have music playing indefinitely. It removes the process of thinking about what to play next. But if you prefer to have control over what you hear, consider turning autoplay off for peace of mind.
