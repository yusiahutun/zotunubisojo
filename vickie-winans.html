Early Life

Vickie Winans was born on October 18, 1953, in Michigan, USA. She is currently 70 years old. Her zodiac sign is Libra. Winans’s mother is Mattie A. Bowman who is a housewife and her father is Aaron Bowman who worked at various times as a laborer, contractor, carpenter, and mason.

Moreover, she began singing in church at the age of eight, and as a teenager, she sang with a group known as the International Sounds of Deliverance.

Career

Vickie Winans is a famous American gospel singer. Her marriage to Marvin led Winans to consider a music career. Hence, she was asked to and she joined Winans Part II. This group included then-in-laws BeBe and CeCe Winans and brother Daniel Winans.

Nevertheless, the group never took off as BeBe and CeCe left to join the PTL Singers of the long-running Christian television show, The PTL Club.

Moreover, the Winans became the best-selling act on their record label, Light Records, and this enabled her husband Marvin to secure her a recording contract. Also, her solo album, Be Encouraged was released in 1987 and became a major gospel hit.

Nonetheless, the LP included a cover of Dottie Rambo’s, “We Shall Behold Him”, as well as “First Trumpet Sound,” a gospelized remake of Gladys Knight & The Pips’ “Midnight Train To Georgia.”

Furthermore, Be Encouraged netted a Grammy nomination for Best Contemporary Soul Gospel Album, a Stellar Award for Album of the Year, and an Excellence Award for Best Female Contemporary Artist.

Personal Life

Winans has been married and divorced three times. Winans married the late Bishop Ronald E. Brown of Faith Tabernacle Deliverance Temple in Orangeburg, South Carolina after high school.

The couple had a son named Mario Brown. Later, in June 1978, she married Marvin Winans of the gospel group The Winans. They had a son named Marvin Jr.

Moreover, Winans and Marvin Sr. divorced after 16 years of marriage in 1995. She married businessman Joe McLemore at the end of 2003. However, this marriage lasted just a few years before Vickie divorced him.
