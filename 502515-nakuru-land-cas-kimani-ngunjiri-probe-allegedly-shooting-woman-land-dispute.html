Lands CAS Kimani Ngunjiri Allegedly Shoots, Injures Man over Land Dispute in Nakuru

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, May 20, 2024
  • Lands Chief Administrative Secretary Kimani Ngunjiri reportedly stormed a farm belonging to Susan Murugi
  • The former Bahati lawmaker was confronted by the landowner before firing and injuring one man
  • Nakuru CCIO Adnan Ahmed said police had launched investigations into the incident after disarming the Lands CAS

Lands Chief Administrative Secretary (CAS) Kimani Ngunjiri has found himself in trouble following a gun drama in Bahati, Nakuru County, over a land dispute.

The former Bahati MP reportedly stormed a farm owned by Susan Murugi with a tractor and destroyed a fence to adjacent land.

Video of Ngunjiri's shooting incident

In a video seen by TUKO.co.ke, Murugi confronted the Lands CAS, prompting him to shoot three times, injuring one man in the process.

"I raised an alarm attracting the attention of other residents who came to find out but unfortunately the CAS started shooting," said Murugi.

Nakuru CCIO Adnan Ahmed said police had launched investigations into the incident, Nation reported.

The police disarmed the former lawmaker by recovering his registered pistol, which had eight bullets for ballistic examination.

“The matter is pending under probe after one person was injured. We have confiscated the pistol for now,” said Rift Valley head of DCI Francis Wanjau.

Bahati named in probe of Stunchburry's murder

This is not the first time the outspoken Lands CAS is gotten entangled in a gun drama.

In 2021, the former Bahati MP was named as a person of interest in the death of environmental activist Joanna Stuchburry.

Investigations into the murder of the environmental activist revealed she had a confrontation with Ngunjiri before her death.

Stuchburry, renowned for her tireless efforts to conserve the vast Kiambu forest, was shot dead by unknown people on Thursday, July 15, 2021, at her home.

Before her killing, then Environment CS Keriako Tobiko said that the deceased had managed to scuttle the plans of Agropack Limited to create a road through the forest.

Agropack Limited, of which Kimani is the director, intended to create an easement road to connect the lawmaker's parcel of land.
