Another East African Country Fields Candidate to Challenge Raila Odinga's AUC Bid

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, July 27, 2024
  • Raila Odinga now has two opponents from East Africa against his bid for the African Union Commission (AUC) chairmanship
  • Djibouti announced the candidature of its Foreign Affairs Minister Mahamoud Ali Youssouf on Tuesday, April 9
  • Youssouf became Raila's second opponent from the region after Somalia fronted its former minister for Foreign Affairs, Fawzia Adam, in March

Kai Eli, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Djibouti has fronted a candidate in the oncoming African Union Commission (AUC) chairmanship vote.

Who is the third East African candidate for AUC chairperson post?

The vote -attracting three East African candidates so far- is set to be held in February 2025.

Djibouti fielded its Foreign Affairs Minister Mahamoud Ali Youssouf to face off with Kenya's Raila Odinga and Fawzia Adam of Somalia.

The country's presidential office announced Youssouf's candidature in an official communique published on Tuesday, April 9.

The Djibouti minister became Raila's second East African opponent after the latter declared his bid on February 15.

Youssouf's entry into the race posed a threat to Raila's chances who had banked on the regional support to take the post.

The next AUC chairperson is set to come from East Africa following a decision by the AU Executive Council.

Countries from Northen Africa would produce the deputy chairperson, with the remainder regions; Central, Southern and Western Africa producing the six commissioners.

Who is Raila's challenger from Somalia?

In March, Somalia announced the candidature of its former minister for Foreign Affairs, Fawzia Adam.

Whilst Raila had the support of the Kenyan government, Fawzia was equally backed by the Federal Republic of Somalia government under President Hassan Mohamud.

Should Fawzia's bid succeed, she would be the second woman at the helm of the African Union secretariat.

The bid came two years after she vied for the presidency in Somalia; Mohamud won the vote in May 2022.

Dlamini-Zuma, a renowned South African anti-apartheid activist and former government minister, is the first woman to have been the AU Commission chairperson.

Zuma served in the post between 2012 and 2017; she was succeeded by Moussa Faki, the current chairperson whose tenure ends in 2025 after serving two terms.

After Zuma, Kenya's former Foreign Affairs minister Amina Mohamed was on the verge of being the second woman to hold the post.

But Faki routed her after seven rounds of voting in Addis Ababa.

Meanwhile, Raila has the backing of over six presidents.

How many presidents are behind Raila's AU bid?

Already backing him are Kenya's President William Ruto, Yoweri Museveni of Uganda, Samia Suluhu (Tanzania), Felix Tshisekedi (Democratic Republic of Congo), Cyril Ramaphosa of South Africa, South Sudan's Salva Kir, and Rwanda's Paul Kagame.

Ruto has been instrumental in selling Raila's bid to his counterparts.
