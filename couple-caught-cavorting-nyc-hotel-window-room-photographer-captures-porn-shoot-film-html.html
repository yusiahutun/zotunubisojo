Couple are caught cavorting NYC hotel window as in-room photographer captures 'porn shoot' on film

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, May 22, 2024

Visitors to the High Line park in New York on a sunny Sunday afternoon got more than they bargained for - as they were met with some sexual shenanigans through the floor-to-ceiling windows of the trendy Standard Hotel.

A naked man could be seen with his rear virtually against the glass while a clothed photographer could also be seen inside the room, filming the action of a couple of randy hotel guests in what appeared to have been a porn shoot. 

The limbs of a third person could also be seen flailing around as the performance got underway, in full view of people walking down below.

When The Standard Hotel opened in 2009, it quickly developed a reputation as being the perfect platform for exhibitionists to show off. 

Guests at the 18-story hotel, where rooms can be booked from $229-a-night, would intentionally fail to close the curtains as they frolicked naked in front of their rooms' huge windows and it appears such behavior is still continuing.

Naked and semi-dressed staff members were even encouraged to pose in front of the windows in an attempt to generate buzz for the venue providing passersby with an unexpected peep show.

Shocked onlookers have reported seeing men masturbating, professional porn films being shot, and couples engaging in sex in full view of park-goers below.

The High Line park opened atop an old elevated railway in the city's trendy Meatpacking District in 2009.

Although the hotel has not commented on this latest display, in the past the property said managers would try to 'remind guests of the transparency' of the windows.

But when 337-room Standard opened it wasn't shy about the possibilities its enormous windows offered it exhibitionist guests and seemed to cheekily encourage it.

'We'll put up with your banging if you'll put up with ours,' declared one ad while construction work took place at the hotel.

The hotel also asks guests to share their 'intimate and explicit photos with us - those floor to ceiling windows aren't just for the views,' they joked.

'When we built The Standard, we hoped it would provide the best views of New York looking out. We didn't anticipate that it would also provide those views looking in,' managers said in a statement at the time. 

'I've seen men and women, women and women, men and men [in the windows],' Ricky Serling told NYMag when the hotel first opened. 'Lights, leather, chains. Everything.'
