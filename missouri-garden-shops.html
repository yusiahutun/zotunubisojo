Missouri Gardening Shops and Nurseries

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, March 18, 2024

It's time to start sowing seeds, prepping beds, and getting your hands dirty! There are loads of amazing nurseries in Missouri where you'll find plants and inspiration for your plot—one's even been around for nearly 200 years. Here are 10 of Country Living readers's favorite gardening resources in the state:

1. Bowood Farms in Saint Louis, Missouri: "Charming and urban, with a great cafe Café Osage for breakfast or lunch," says Erin Callier McCarthye of the Central West End shop (above) that's housed in a 1920s warehouse and grows most of its plants at a fourth-generation family farm in Clarksville.

2. Missouri Botanical Garden in Saint Louis, Missouri: The nation's oldest botanical garden in continuous operation regularly hosts plant sales, and as Margaret Booker says, "The garden shop here is a treasure."

3. Stark Bro's Nurseries & Orchards in Louisiana, Missouri: The Stark family has been growing apple trees in Missouri since 1816, and their garden center still offers those, along with other fruit and nut trees, berries, roses, and more.

4. Vintage Hill Farm in Franklin, Missouri: This 100-acre farm offers more than 1,700 plant varieties. "They have the most beautiful rose selection," says Michelle Dorman.

5. Soil Service Garden Center & Nursery in Kansas City, Missouri: "Family owned since 1934, with a very helpful staff, good prices, a wide selection, and great quality plants," says Rebecca Joy.

6. Stanley's Garden Center in Saint James, Missouri: Come here for 40 tomato varieties and 20 kinds of peppers—plus stone, mulch, and other supplies, too.

7. Homestead Farms Nursery in Owensville, Missouri: "I go every year for hostas," says Susi Reed Junge of this mail-order nursery that welcomes walk-up customers during the growing season. Along with hostas, Homestead specializes in daylilies, peonies, siberian iris, ferns, and clematis.

8. Springwater Greenhouse & Landscaping in Marshall, Missouri: Two friends started this business nearly 20 years ago, and it's grown to include six greenhouses.

9. Farrand Farms in Kansas City, Missouri: "It's family owned and a joy to visit," says Karen Mehl. "The displays are eye-catching and really draw you in."

10. Baker Creek Heirloom Seed Co. in Mansfield, Missouri: Jere and Emilee Gettle stock seeds for 1,600 varieties of non-hybrid, non-GMO vegetables, flowers, and herbs.

Have a favorite garden center or nursery in your state? Share your secret with the hashtag #clstatesecret.
