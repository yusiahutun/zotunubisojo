18 Drinking Songs - Best Party Songs

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, June 17, 2024

Throwing a house party is a pretty big responsibility, but trying to appease every guest’s musical taste is a whole other undertaking. Fear not; we’ve rounded up the some of the best songs about drinking—from a smooth pairing of Amy Winehouse and Nas, to the insane vocal recordings of Lil Jon—to make a playlist that pretty much everyone can agree to (or at least drink to).

“raingurl” by Yaeji

Yaeji’s subdued, straightforward vocal delivery on “raingurl” brings this dance club track to another level of addicting. Plus, “Mother Russia in my cup” has to be one of the best known euphemisms about a glass full of straight vodka.

Download

“Swimming Pools (Drank)” by Kendrick Lamar

This good kid, m.A.A.d. City cut is considered one of the first tracks that fully threw Kendrick into mainstream superstardom. On the surface, “Swimming Pools (Drank)” is a club banger about heavy drinking—but really, it’s a cloaked testimonial about the pressures that often come along with the alcohol intake.

Download

“Can’t Hold My Liquor” by Kanye West feat. Justin Vernon and Chief Keef

On the increasingly self-aware “Can’t Hold My Liquor” Kanye, Chief Keef, and Bon Iver’s Vernon come together as an unlikely trio owning up to their consumption habits.

Download

“Blame it” by Jamie Foxxx feat. T-Pain

Throw on “Blame It” and cue a drunk sing-along to the chopped-up and autotuned “a-aa-a-al-c-ohol” blaring in the chorus.

Download

“Pine and Ginger” by Amindi K Fro$t, Tessellated, & Valleyz

Consider this your immediate sonic trip to the islands and a lounge chair.

Download

“Tipsy” by J-Kwon

“Teen drinking is very bad... but I got a fake ID though” is an opening line that will live in infamy, as it clearly resonates in our middle school and high school memories. However, looking back, we can't say we condone the teen drinking and whatever comes along with it.

Download

“Gin and Juice” by Snoop Dogg feat. Daz Dillinger

The ultimate West Coast drinking anthem.

Download

“Cherry Wine” by Nas feat. Amy Winehouse

Drinking songs don’t necessarily have to be about binge-consuming liquor in a crowded room full of strangers standing on a sticky beer-soaked floor. For something a little more intimate, throw on this smooth Amy Winehouse and Nas collab, and pour up some cherry wine.

Download

“Pour It Up” by Rihanna

The only thing better than a good drink is making money, and no one knows that better than BadGal RiRi herself.

Download

“Can’t Say” by Travis Scott feat. Don Toliver

While this pick is as much about taking someone home as it is about drinking, there’s something entrancing about the pairing of Travis Scott and fellow Houston rapper Don Toliver leaning into some heavy autotune.

Download

“Tequila” by The Champs

This hit sounds about as unhinged as a night of drinking actual tequila can make you feel.

Download

“Act a Fool” by Lil Jon and Three 6 Mafia

Lil Jon was known for carrying a chalice deemed “the pimp cup” to pretty much every function in the early 2000s, so it’s only right that he has an entire song about drinking a lot and subsequently going wild.

“All Night” by Chance the Rapper feat. Knox Fortune

With some added help from Chicago singer/producer Knox Fortune on the chorus, “All Night” is a campy, upbeat ode to long nights of dancing, partying, and trying to keep everyone in line.

Download

“Friends in Low Places” by Garth Brooks

Garth Brooks fully leans into the bass in his voice for this classic homage to his pals, the ultimate drinking buddies.

“Drunk in Love” by Beyoncé

For the majority of the beginning of their careers, Beyoncé and Jay-Z were pretty hush on their personal relationship. But that took a turn later on, resulting in tracks like “Drunk In Love,” which finds Bey singing some provocative bars about tipsy late nights with her man.

Download

“One Beer” by MF Doom

On a project dedicated entirely to his favorite foods, MF Doom made some room for track dedicated to his number one beverage.

Download

“Drunk Girls” by LCD Soundsystem

“Drunk Girls” is somewhat of a goofy, drunk-inspired departure for the otherwise fairly serious men of LCD Soundsystem.

Download

“Brass Monkey” by Beastie Boys

There are plenty of theories floating around on what “Brass Monkey” is really about, but apparently, the vague phrase is in reference to a slew of lower-level cocktails. Cheers to the Beastie Boys for bringing this obscure alcoholic mix to the American public.

Download
