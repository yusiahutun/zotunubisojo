7 Best Bras for Every Figure That Fit Like Magic

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, March 14, 2024

Finding a bra that fits perfectly, flatters your figure, and is comfortable even after hours of wear feels a little bit like a magic trick — and like magic, it can feel impossible. That’s why we’ve rounded up some of the best bras that can fix everything from the dreaded “quad boob” to armpit bulges and back rolls.

Whether your breasts are surrendering to gravity’s pull, pointing in different directions, or deflating like balloons the day after the party, there’s a bra that can make magic happen for you. Just think of us as your fairy godmother!

But first, to find the right fit …

How to Buy a Bra Online

The idea of buying a bra without trying it on first can be daunting. This handy guide will help you zero in on the right size, no fitting room needed!

1) Measure the band size

While wearing a bra, wrap a soft measuring tape around the area where the bra band sits. Round the number found to the nearest whole number of inches. The goal is for the band to fit comfortably on the loosest hook, so it can be tightened over time as the bra loses elasticity.

2) Measure the bust size

Wrap the measuring tape loosely around the fullest part of the chest (near nipple level); round to the nearest whole number of inches. Tip: It’s best to wear a non-push-up, lightly lined bra when measuring so breasts are as close to their natural shape and size as possible.

3) Calculate the cup size

Subtract the band size from the bust size, then take that number and calculate as follows to figure out your true cup size:

  • One inch difference = A cup
  • Two inch difference = B cup
  • Three inch difference = C cup
  • Four inch difference = D cup
  • Five inch difference = DD cup
  • Six inch difference = DDD cup

When bra cups fit properly, they will lie flat against skin, sans gaping or spillage.
