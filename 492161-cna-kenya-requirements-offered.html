CNA course in Kenya requirements and where it is offered

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, April 15, 2024

Unlike most nursing and medical jobs, certified nursing assistants don't need to earn a university or college degree. However, one must earn a certificate or diploma in the field to be equipped with the appropriate skills in personal patient care, communication, vital signs, mental health and much more. In Kenya, nursing assistants are in high demand. So, where can you pursue a CNA course in Kenya, and what are the admission requirements?

What does it mean to be a certified nurse assistant?

People in the medical field are interdependent. A nurse often plays a major role in health care as they assist in numerous areas. A certified nurse assistant helps patients with immediate healthcare needs, supervised by a senior nurse.

Of course, healthcare centres will have different requirements and responsibilities for their nursing assistants, but the bottom line is that they are there to help. They ascertain that patients are well-cared for and safe during their hospital stay.

Some of the major responsibilities of a certified nurse assistant include the following:

  • Gathering medical supplies.
  • Turning and moving patients.
  • Take vital signs.
  • Provide companionship.
  • Safety procedures.
  • Taking care of wounds.
  • Grooming patients.
  • Assist with medical procedures.
  • Transporting patients.
  • Documenting information.

Most of the roles of a certified nurse assistant are categorized as activities of daily living, but they can be more than that. Considering the changing patient needs, it is highly unlikely that every day will be the same when you get a job as a nursing aide. It is also important to note that the role and responsibilities of nursing aides alter by the environment.

Nurse assistant course requirements in Kenya

To qualify for a certificate or diploma in nursing aide in Kenya, one must meet the minimum requirements for admission to any of their preferred learning institution. One must have attained a mean grade of E in their Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education.

They can also qualify if they have equivalent qualifications determined by Kenya National Qualification Authority. Other learning institutions have different requirements for admission. Some require one to have attained a KCSE mean grade of C- (minus) and have attained D+ in Mathematics or Commerce and C in English.

Where can you study CNA in Kenya?

The best way to qualify and practice as a nurse aide in Kenya is to undergo the course in accredited learning institutions. And if you feel that you have what it takes, you can also opt for a short medical course.
