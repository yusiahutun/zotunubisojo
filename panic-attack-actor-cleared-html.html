Panic attack actor cleared | Daily Mail Online

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, July 30, 2024

A film and television actor was cleared today of being drunk on an aircraft after prosecutors offered no evidence against him.

Ciaran McMenamin was accused of committing the offence on board a flight from Los Angeles to Heathrow.

The 29-year-old, of Therapia Road, East Dulwich, south London, was arrested at the end of the journey in November but said today his behaviour had been caused by a panic attack.

The actor, who is originally from Enniskillen, in Northern Ireland, appeared today at London's Isleworth Crown Court for the start of a trial lasting up to three days.

Not guilty

But Judge Andrew McDowall formally entered a not guilty verdict against him after the Crown decided not to proceed with the case.

The judge had declined an application by the prosecution barrister Fer Chinner to delay the trial in order to call an expert witness, saying lawyers had "missed the boat" on making their application to challenge the defence's medical evidence.

Jerry Hayes, for the defendant, had argued against doing so as his client had already had to turn down acting work with the case hanging over him and was also burdened by the costs of lawyers and medical experts. Witnesses had also had to be flown over from Belfast for the case.

After the judge's decision Miss Chinner told the court: "In light of the fact that the Crown are not in a position to conduct any meaningful cross-examination of either of the two doctors because the Crown cannot instruct their own experts to deal with the suggestions in this document it seems that there is no realistic prospect of a conviction and for that reason I intend to offer no evidence."

Mr McMenamin, who appeared in court today wearing a smart dark blue pinstripe suit and striped black and grey tie, has appeared in numerous television and film roles.

They include a TV adaptation of David Copperfield and the film To End All Wars alongside Robert Carlyle, Kiefer Sutherland and Long Firm star Mark Strong.

Delighted

Outside court, Mr Hayes said: "Mr McMenamin is absolutely delighted that justice has been done and he leaves this court without any stain on his character.

"Also, he can get on with his life and earn some money."

He added that he hoped air crews would in future be trained to spot the difference between those suffering from a panic attack and those who were drunk.

Panic

The actor had fallen asleep on board the flight and woken up "confused" Mr Hayes said.

"He was abusive to some of the air crew but it wasn't deliberate," he added.

"I suspect he will be more careful next time he travels by air."

Mr McMenamin said: "It is quite a common syndrome. There are various types of panic disorder.

"You wake up in a confused state and don't know where you are. It is something that happens to me every couple of months."

He still faces a summary charge of threatening, abusive and insulting behaviour but Mr Hayes said he would be "very surprised" if prosecutors now pressed ahead with the allegations, which would be heard at a magistrates' court.

Reacting to today's result the actor said: "I am delighted. It's been hanging over me for a while. Now I can just get on with my life, hopefully."
