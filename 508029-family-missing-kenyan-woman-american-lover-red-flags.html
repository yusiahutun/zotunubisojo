Family of Missing Kenyan Woman in US Says They Never Liked her American Lover: &quot;Red Flags&quot;

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, January 26, 2024
  • Irene Gakwa's seemingly organised life took a different turn when she met and fell in love with fiancé Nathan Hightman from Craigslist
  • She went missing in 2022, after which Hightman started using her bank details to pay for his bills and shop without reporting her disappearance
  • Family members have now disclosed that they did not like the man their daughter fell in love with because something about him seemed odd, but they couldn't do anything

The family of Kenyan Irene Gakwa who went missing in 2022 shortly after meeting her fiancé on Craigslist has opened up about their feelings for their kin's lover.

Following the disappearance of the Wyoming-based woman, lover Nathan Hightman was arrested by the police.

Hightman not only failed to report his girlfriend as missing but also went ahead and used her bank details to pay bills and shop.

The arrest was made after he admitted that instead of contacting the police, he stayed back and fleeced her accounts after she went missing.

Family disliked Hightman

According to family members, the man their daughter fell in love with behaved in a manner that raised their eyebrows, but they could do little to change her decision.

Gakwa had relocated from Kenya to Idaho and enrolled into nursing school before meeting and falling in love with Hightman through Craigslist.

”I will say the truth. I mean, I really didn’t like him, but you know, what do you do?” the missing woman's brother Christopher Gakwa said, as reported by NewsNation.

Trouble checked into paradise when Gakwa and Hightman moved from Idaho to Gillette, Wyoming and she cut communication with her family.

Relationship had fell on hard times

The last her family in Nairobi saw her was through a video call on February 24, 2022, after which her calls stopped and her texts, often in Swahili, turned to English only.

“Not communicating with her dad, to me, that was the biggest red flag, because I know the relationship that they have, they are super close,” another relative named Githu said.

When Gakwa’s brothers drove 800 miles to Gillette to check on their sister, Hightman told them that the 32-year-old had packed her clothes into two plastic bags and left in a dark-colored SUV.

In the days leading to her disappearance, her relationship with Hightman had become strained, a development that she mentioned to her family members in the US about.
