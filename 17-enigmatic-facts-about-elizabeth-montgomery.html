Elizabeth Montgomery was born on April 15, 1933.

Elizabeth Montgomery, the talented actress known for her iconic role as Samantha Stephens in the TV series “Bewitched,” was born on April 15, 1933, in Los Angeles, California.

She came from a famous Hollywood family.

Elizabeth Montgomery was the daughter of renowned actor Robert Montgomery and Broadway actress Elizabeth Allen. She was destined to follow in their footsteps and make a name for herself in the entertainment industry.

“Bewitched” brought her international fame.

One of the most successful sitcoms in television history, “Bewitched” catapulted Elizabeth Montgomery to worldwide recognition. Her portrayal of the lovable witch Samantha Stephens charmed audiences for eight seasons.

Elizabeth Montgomery had a unique nose twitch.

In the TV series “Bewitched,” Samantha Stephens would often use her magical powers by twitching her nose. Elizabeth Montgomery’s skillful nose twitch became one of the show’s most memorable elements.

She was an advocate for women’s rights.

Elizabeth Montgomery was a staunch supporter of women’s rights and equality. She used her platform to advocate for various causes, including the Equal Rights Amendment and reproductive rights.

Elizabeth Montgomery had a successful stage career.

While primarily known for her television work, Elizabeth Montgomery also had a thriving career in theater. She starred in Broadway productions such as “The Miracle Worker” and “Late Love.”

She had a passion for photography.

Elizabeth Montgomery had a deep love for photography and often captured beautiful moments behind the lens. Her photographs were exhibited in galleries and published in magazines.

Elizabeth Montgomery was married four times.

Throughout her life, Elizabeth Montgomery experienced four marriages. Her husbands included Frederick Gallatin Cammann, Gig Young, William Asher (who also directed “Bewitched”), and Robert Foxworth.

Elizabeth Montgomery had three children.

From her marriage to William Asher, Elizabeth Montgomery had three children: William, Robert, and Rebecca Asher. Her children followed in her creative footsteps and pursued careers in the entertainment industry.

She was a dedicated animal rights activist.

Elizabeth Montgomery was a passionate advocate for animal rights and worked alongside organizations such as PETA and the Humane Society to promote ethical treatment of animals.

Elizabeth Montgomery received numerous award nominations.

Throughout her career, Elizabeth Montgomery received several award nominations. She was recognized for her outstanding performances with Emmy nominations and Golden Globe nominations.

She was known for her versatility as an actress.

Elizabeth Montgomery showcased her versatility as an actress by taking on diverse roles in both television and film. From drama to comedy, she effortlessly transitioned between genres.
