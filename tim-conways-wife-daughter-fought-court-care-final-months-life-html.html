Tim Conway's wife and daughter fought in court about his care for the final months of his life

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, June 23, 2024

Actor Tim Conway, (pictured), died Tuesday morning in a Los Angeles care facility

Actor Tim Conway's wife and daughter were involved in a legal battle over his care in the final months of his life before his death on Tuesday.

The Emmy award-winning comedian's wife Charlene and daughter Kelly, eventually settled a dispute over his medical care in March this year.  

In August, Kelly, who's mother is Conway's first wife Mary Ann Dalton,  filed documents to become named her father’s conservator and to take control of his treatment.

This included giving him his medications for dementia. She simultaneously asked for a temporary restraining order against her step-mother Charlene.

However lawyers for both parties confirmed Charlene's appointment as her 85-year-old husband's conservator, with the action first reported by PEOPLE.  

Conway died Tuesday morning in a Los Angeles care facility, according to Howard Bragman, who heads LaBrea Media.  

Charlene Conway, and a daughter, Jackie, were at his side. The cause was a disorder in which there is an excess of fluid on the brain, Bragman said.

Kelly Conway, (left), the daughter of actor Tim Conway, (center), and his wife Charlene Fusco, (right), were involved in a legal dispute over his medical care in the final months of his life  

Kelly disagreed with Charlene’s plan to 'move [Tim] out of the excellent skilled nursing facility he is currently at' and admit him somewhere without 'registered nurses at all times and his 24-hour caregiver and speech therapist [to help with swallowing],' PEOPLE reported last August.

She claimed that her father was 'almost entirely unresponsive, adding that he could no longer 'properly provide for his personal needs for physical health, food, and clothing.'

The legal fight continued when Fusco accused Kelly of having relied on a false medical record and breached her father's right to medical privacy, according to AOL.com.

Charlene was appointed conservator for her husband in March, after the court found that a conservator for Tim was, in fact, necessary.

A publicist for Kelly told USA Today that she was 'very happy with the outcome after more than a year of fighting to keep her dad safe and sound.'

The statement added: 'She is now allowed to see him and is permitted to meet with Tim’s healthcare professionals.' 

Charlene Fusco was appointed as her 85-year-old husband's conservator in March

Conway and his wife, Mary Anne Dalton, married in 1961 and had six children. The marriage ended in divorce. He later married Charlene Fusco.

In addition to his wife and daughter Jackie, Conway is survived by children Tim Jr., Patrick, Jamie, Kelly, Corey and Seann, as well as two grandchildren, Courtney and Sophia.  

Conway won four Emmy Awards on her TV variety show, starred aboard 'McHale's Navy' and later voiced the role of Barnacle Boy for 'Spongebob Squarepants.'

Burnett said in a statement Tuesday that she was heartbroken. 'He was one in a million, not only as a brilliant comedian but as a loving human being. I cherish the times we had together both on the screen and off. He'll be in my heart forever.'

Conway was best known for his skits on 'The Carol Burnett Show.' He is pictured with Carol Burnett during a gala birthday party for her in Los Angeles in 1986 

A native of Ohio, Conway credited his Midwestern roots for putting him on the right path to laughs, with his deadpan expression and innocent, simple-minded demeanor

Tributes came from across the comedy world, including from Conan O'Brien, who said 'no one made me laugh harder than Tim Conway' and Kathy Griffin.

Griffin called him 'a wildly talented, comedy giant.' Al Roker tweeted out a link to Conway playing a hysterically incompetent dentist.

A native of Ohio, Conway credited his Midwestern roots for putting him on the right path to laughs, with his deadpan expression and innocent, simple-minded demeanor.

He also found success in the 1980s in a series of comedy videos based on an oddly short character named Dorf. Among them were "Dorf on Golf" and "Dorf Goes Fishing."

More recently Conway voiced the role of Barnacle Boy for the hugely popular children's series "SpongeBob SquarePants."

He was born Thomas Conway in 1933 in the Cleveland suburb of Willoughby. He attended Bowling Green State University and served in the U.S. Army. He got his career start on local TV in Cleveland in the 1950s, where his duties included comedy spots on a late-night movie show.
