She is known for her pretty blonde hair and flawless complexion.

And it isn't hard to see where Heroes star Ali Larter gets her good looks from.

The 36-year-old star stepped out with her mother Margaret as they went shopping at Wholefoods in West Hollywood on Friday.

Like mother, like daughter: Ali Larter and her mother Margaret went shopping at Wholefoods in West Hollywood on Friday

During the outing the actress carried a bouquet of flowers while a young man helped with their groceries.

Ali looked simple and chic in a black blazer, white top, black skinny jeans, sandals, and a maroon croc-texture bag, while her mother looked just as stylish in a cream-coloured pencil skirt, a printed blouse and a cardigan.

The actress certainly looked happy during her day out and there are currently plenty of reasons for Ali to have a spring in her step.

Pretty petals: During the outing the actress purchased a bouquet of flowers

Chic: Ali looked simple and chic in a black blazer, white top, black skinny jeans, sandals, and a maroon croc-texture bag

Flawless: With minimal make-up on her complexion and her signature blonde locks pulled back into a ponytail, Ali looked flawless

It was recently announced that she's scored a key role in a pilot by the creator of Homeland.

Ali has been cast as the female lead in Howard Gordon's TNT pilot, Legends, opposite Game of Thrones and Lord of the Rings alum, Sean Bean, 53.

So excited to start shooting #homeland creator @howardmgordon new show #legends! Can't wait to work with that legend Sean Bean & Dave Semel!' the star tweeted to her 24,000 followers, earlier this week.

And on Monday, she Tweeted: 'Working with Ned Stark is like sitting in on a master class,' in a reference to Sheffield-born Bean's Game of Thrones character.

Best of friends: Ali is often spotted out and about with her mother Margaret

The British acting legend is seasoned thespian after being educated at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts.

He stumbling into an acting class while studying to be a welder in Rotherham, Yorkshire.

In the spy show, produced by Homeland's Howard Gordon, Larter will play Crystal, an operative who initially opposes Agent Martin Odum's (Bean) return to their task force.

Supermodel Amber Valletta, Steve Harris, Rob Mayes, and Tina Majorino will also appear in Legends.

Larter, who is married to comedian Hayes MacArthur, will be hoping for a hit after her disappointment with Asset.

The young mother, who gave birth to son Teddy two years ago, enjoyed huge success with Heroes after bagging the role of dual personality, Niki Andrews.
