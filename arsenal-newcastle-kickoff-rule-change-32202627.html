People are only just realising why Arsenal vs Newcastle is at 8pm after Prem rule change

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, August 3, 2024

Arsenal will face Newcastle United in an 8pm kick-off tonight due to a Premier League rule change related to European fixtures.

There are three different kick-off times today (February 24), even though there is no lunchtime game. Four matches will start at 3pm, followed by Manchester City vs Bournemouth at 5.30pm. The day's action will conclude with the Gunners hosting the Magpies, three days on from their late defeat to Porto in the first leg of their Champions League round of 16 tie.

Managers have often complained about the short gap between European and domestic games. Therefore, a new rule has been introduced in the interests of player welfare while honouring the league's TV schedule, reports the Mirror.

The rule says that any club that plays in a European competition on a Wednesday night, like Arsenal did, won't be allowed to play at the usual 12.30pm slot on Saturday. Instead, they will play at 8pm.

As a result, TNT Sports, who usually broadcast the lunchtime game, can still choose their preferred match of the weekend. Without the rule, broadcasters would face limitations on which teams they can and can't pick based on UEFA's scheduling, which often includes the league's biggest clubs.

Newcastle have had to change kick-off times twice this season because of the rule, with their matches against Manchester City and Manchester United both being switched to 8pm on Saturdays.

Arsenal are looking for revenge after they lost at St James' Park in the reverse fixture, with Anthony Gordon scoring a controversial winner which was checked three times by VAR. Furious Gunners boss Mikel Arteta branded the decision to award the goal a "disgrace" and "embarrassing".

Ahead of tonight's game, Arteta was asked if he had seen an improvement in officiating and VAR since his post-match rant, which was endorsed by an official statement from the club.

He replied: “Well, that is what we all wanted. That, at the end, the decisions are better and I think the last stats that came across show that there was a significant improvement and a lot of the decisions they were getting right, so hopefully that is the case and we continue to do that.

“I talked the way I felt [at the time]. I was very straight and I did it in a way that was pretty strong but within the law because I didn’t get charged for it and I think that tells the story.”
