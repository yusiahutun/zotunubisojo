Mitford house gutted by fire after blaze breaks out in drawing room

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, March 30, 2024

By Daily Mail Reporter

Published: 19:18 EDT, 22 April 2012 | Updated: 19:18 EDT, 22 April 2012

1

A manor house built by the Mitfords has been badly damaged in a fire.

The blaze began in the drawing room of sprawling Swinbrook House, on the outskirts of Swinbrook, near Burford, Oxfordshire.

Firemen said it affected ‘a substantial amount of the property’.

The mansion was built in 1926 by the second Baron Redesdale, David Freeman-Mitford, the father of Nancy, Pamela, Diana, Unity, Jessica and Deborah Mitford.

Blaze: Swinbrook House Estate in Oxfordshire before a blaze gutted the house built by the Mitfords

Six people – said to be guests and staff working for the current occupier – were evacuated from the mansion.

Fire crews were greeted with smoke pouring out of the three-storey building and faced a race against time to save the historic property and hundreds of artefacts inside it.

Firefighters with breathing apparatus battled to stop the flames spreading as others worked to save valuable heirlooms within the famous house.

They had been alerted to the fire after a smoke alarm was set off and one of the men inside had attempted to tackle the blaze as he waited for the fire brigade to arrive.

Fame: Diana Mitford, who became the second wife of Sir Oswald Mosley was one of the daughters who lived in the mansion after it was built in 1926

However, the flames were too intsense for him to deal with alone.

'The drawing room was completely destroyed,” said fire incident commander Nick Bourke.

'Approximately 70 per cent of the property was affected by smoke. It is a very large property and a substantial amount of the property was affected.

'It appears to be quite a famous house. It was absolutely beautiful but sadly there will be some items which are beyond repair.

'We did manage to salvage quite a lot of the property however,' he added.

Fire engines from nearby Burford and Witney initially raced to the scene of the blaze and were backed up by crews from Abingdon, Charlbury and Chipping Norton.

'When we arrived we were faced with a large fire in the drawing room but due to the quick actions of the crews, we managed to get the flames under control quite quickly,' said Mr Bourke.

Oxfordshire Fire and Rescue Service was called to the fire just after 8pm last night/on Saturday and were able to bring the blaze under control within two hours.

Crews remained at the scene until late into the night as they tried to establish what could have caused the fire, as well as salvaging items inside the property - which the Mitfords left in 1935.

The Mitford sisters grew up in and around Swinbrook and four of the sisters were buried in the village graveyeard.

The youngest of the clan, now the Dowager Duchess of Devonshire, owns a nearby pub in the village - The Swan - which was left to the Mitford family by the previous owner.
