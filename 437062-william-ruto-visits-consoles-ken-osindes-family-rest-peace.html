William Ruto Visits, Condoles with Ken Osindes Family: Rest in Peace

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, July 28, 2024
  • Deputy President William Ruto visited the late Ken Osinde's family at his Lavington home in Nairobi on Tuesday evening
  • On Sunday, December 12, Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i joined other leaders in mourning Osinde
  • Matiang'i expressed sorrow at the untimely death of his friend, whom he noted exhibited no illness or sickness until his passing on

Deputy President William Ruto has condoled with the family of his Chief of Staff Ken Osinde following his demise.

The second in command visited Osinde's family at his Lavington home in Nairobi on Tuesday evening.

"At Lavington in Nairobi county, offered our prayers and words of comfort to the family of my late Chief of Staff, Ken Nyauncho Osinde. May God grant them strength at this painful time. Rest In Peace, Ken," Ruto wrote on his official Twitter account after visiting the family.

Fred Matiang'i mourned Osinde

On Sunday, December 12, TUKO.co.ke reported Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i mourned Osinde.

The CS expressed sorrow at the untimely death of his friend, whom he noted exhibited no illness or sickness until his passing on.

"I am utterly confounded and shocked by the news of the sudden death of Ambassador Ken Osinde. For the duration I have known him, Ambassador Osinde has never exhibited sickness or ill health. Indeed, his life has been a portrait of robust health. His devotion to a rigorous personal fitness regime is public knowledge...My heartfelt condolences go to his wife, Judy, his family, friends and the Public Service that has lost. an illustrious administrator," the CS mourned.

Osinde in Makueni

Barely three weeks ago, Osinde represented Ruto at the closing ceremony of the 7th Devolution Conference in Makueni county.

Osinde, however, was turned away by governors who faulted the DP for being disrespectful.

Ruto had been slated to preside over the closing ceremony of the event held in Makueni county on Friday, November 26.

However, the deputy president snubbed the event, instead sending his Chief of Staff, Osinde, to represent him.

Osinde was stopped from presenting Ruto's speech, the governors saying, "the event is an international one and not everyone can preside over the closing ceremony".
