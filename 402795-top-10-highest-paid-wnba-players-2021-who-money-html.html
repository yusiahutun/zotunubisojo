Top 10 highest-paid WNBA players in 2021: Who makes the most money?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, August 26, 2024

The Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA) has undergone massive upheaval and accumulated immense popularity since its formulation in 1996. Today, female, well-built and talented professional players are gradually getting famous as their male counterparts, getting endorsements and major brand deals. However, although facing an upward trajectory in recent times, the salary is a fraction of what the NBA players receive. Nonetheless, the new eight-year collective bargaining agreement signed in January 2020 guarantees the players a significant increase. Who are the highest-paid WNBA players in 2021?

WNBA players' collective bargaining agreement increased the salary cap, which means more significant contracts in the coming years. The league has found its footing in the 25 years of its existence but significant changes are expected to upgrade it to the men's league standards. Who makes the most money in the WNBA?

Highest-paid WNBA players in 2021

Who is the highest-paid WNBA player of all time? Indeed, it is hard figuring it out, considering its low-key nature. It is as enjoyable as men's and holds immensely talented professional players yearning for a breakthrough in their careers.

Although women professional basketball players are not as many and well-paid as those in the NBA, they earn a sizeable amount slated to rise. A listing showing the highest-paid women's basketball player would likely provide great insight.

1. Liz Cambage (Las Vegas Aces) – $500,000

Liz Cambage plays for the Las Vegas Aces and is probably the highest-paid WNBA player of all time. The Australian is one of the league's most prominent critics. She often slams the pay disparity with the NBA and advocates for better treatment of players like their counterparts in Asia and Europe.

Under the new WNBA agreement, which includes a 53 percent increase in total cash compensation for players, she is expected to earn more than $500,000. Liz currently holds the WNBA single-game scoring record with her 53-point performance against the New York Liberty in July 2018.

2. Elena Delle Donne (Washington Mystics) – $221,450

Elena Delle plays for the Washington Mystics. She signed a four year/$899,480 contract with the team that includes an annual salary of $224,870 in 2021. The compensation has a cap hit of $221,450.

The 31-year-old female professional basketball player was in 2014 named the ambassador for Special Olympics.

On top of that, Ellena has won two WNBA Most Valuable Player Awards (2015 and 2019) and became the first WNBA player to join the 50-40-90 club. She has not played much professionally overseas, only taking part in the regular basketball season in the U.S.

3. DeWanna Bonner(Connecticut Sun) - $215,000

DeWanna Bonner is one of the most talented WNBA players that spent 10 seasons with the Phoenix Mercury. It was during this time she exhibited exemplary basketball skills and became a WNBA superstar.

Today, she plays for the Connecticut Sun after becoming an unrestricted free agent capable of signing with any team. Bonner joined the Phoenix in a sign-and-trade deal. She could no longer receive core play designation from the team as she was cored the league maximum of four times.

When playing for the Phoenix Mercury, DeWanna Bonner scored in double figures in most matches and was named in three WNBA All-Star Games in 2015, 2018, and 2019. Additionally, she won two championships in 2009 and 2014.

Under the new CBA, DeWanna can hit the full maximum salary and now earns an estimated $215,000. She likely enjoys the highest WNBA salary compared to other female basketball players.

4. Emma Meesseman (Washington Mystics) – $215,000

Emma Meesseman that currently plays for Washington Mystics earns a salary of around $215,000. She signed a one-year deal with Mystics in the offseason that increased her earnings significantly.

She is a Belgian professional basketball player drafted by the Mystics with the 19th overall pick in the second round of the 2013 WNBA draft. At 27 years old, Emma is in her prime and is likely to remain one of the highest-paid WNBA players and most versatile frontcourt athletes.

5. Skylar Diggins-Smith (Phoenix Mercury) – $215,000

Skylar Diggins-Smith is a skilled American professional basketballer that plays for the Phoenix Mercury in the WNBA. She was drafted third overall by the Tulsa Shock in the 2013 WNBA draft.

During her high school days, Skylar Diggins-Smith won the National Gatorade Player of the Year, the Gatorade Female Athlete of the Year, and McDonald's All-American.

Currently, Diggins has signed a four-year deal for the new league maximum via the Phoenix Mercury sign-and-trade. This makes her possess the biggest WNBA contract comparable to other players in her stature in the league. She receivess the maximum salary of $215,000.

6. Brittney Griner (Phoenix Mercury) – $215,000

Centre Brittney Griner plays for the Phoenix Mercury that gave her core player designation in January 2020. She has been earning a base salary of $215,000 under her multiyear contract with the team.

The talented player lived up to her parting words at the culmination of the 2019 Phoenix Mercury centre where she stated:

They want me back; I plan on signing back. If I don't play here, I won't play anywhere (in the WNBA). I mean that. I'm not playing for any other organization. I'm a Phoenix Mercury until I'm done.

The 29-year-old is now committed to the team alongside other skilled players like Diana Taurasi and Skylar Diggins-Smith under a four-year contract. Griner has been a pivotal part of Phoenix Mercury, making the first team in 2014 and 2019. She holds a gold medal playing for the United States of America in the 2016 Olympics.

7. Sue Bird (Seattle Storm) – $215,000

Sue Bird is another WNBA player to receive the top WNBA salary, joining the best-paid female athletes in the league. She is one of the oldest players in the league and one of the fiercest as she plays her role pretty well.

Sue underwent knee surgery in 2019 and didn't play in that year's season. She has represented the Seattle Storm for more than 17 years in the WNBA and earns the maximum amount of $215,000. Sue extended her contract with the team that guaranteed her the amount as her annual average salary.

Bird has shown exemplary performance in the WNBA, and she seems ageless. That is why she is one of the highest-paid WNBA players.

8. Courtney Vandersloot (Chicago Sky) – $203,000

Courtney Vandersloot is an American-Hungarian basketball player that plays as a point guard for the Chicago Sky. She has terrific achievements since her college years. Courtney is the only woman at Gonzaga University in West Coast Conference to be named the conference’s player of the year three times. She earns a salary of $203,000 playing for Chicago Sky.

Although Allie’s is not the WNBA highest salary, she earns a considerable amount based on the recent revisions in player's wages. She gets a remuneration of $194, 000 from Chicago Sky. Allie extended her contract by signing a one-year deal in 2019 that increased her earnings to $117,500 in the 2019 season.

10. Alisha Gray (Dallas Wings) – $160,000

Alisha Gray is the shooting guard for the Dallas Wings. She was chosen to join the team in the WNBA draft of 2017 as the fourth overall pick. She is reportedly receiving $160,000 as her annual salary.
