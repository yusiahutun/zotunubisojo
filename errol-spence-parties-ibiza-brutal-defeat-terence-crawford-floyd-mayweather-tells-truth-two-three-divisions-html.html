Errol Spence parties in Ibiza after brutal defeat by Terence Crawford... as Floyd Mayweather tells '

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, April 19, 2024

Errol Spence Jr. has been spotted partying in Ibiza just a little more than a week after losing to Terence Crawford, as all-time great Floyd Mayweather claims that the ex-welterweight champion should jump up two or three weight classes. 

Spence Jr., 33, shared a mirror selfie of himself with long-time girlfriend Ashra Ortiz by his side from the Spanish party island on Tuesday, just 10 days after having lost to Crawford by a ninth-round technical knockout in Dallas, Texas. 

And the pair's high-profile undisputed 147-pound championship matchup has made Mayweather think that Spence should try competing at either '160 or 168.' 


The Truth is managed by Premier Boxing Champions' Al Haymon, who also undersaw Mayweather's glittering career from 1996 to 2017, during which the 46-year-old went undefeated and won 15 major world championships. Mayweather, however, through threw a jab at the man he once called his 'second father.'

'With Errol Spence being under the PBC banner, which a lot of times you see fighters say, ''I'm with PBC. I'm with Al Haymon - but I'm not with Floyd Mayweather,''' Money told FightHype.com. 'And I'm not talking about Errol. I'm just talking about in general.

Errol Spence Jr. has been recovering from his July fight with Terence Crawford in Ibiza with his long-time girlfriend, Ashra Ortiz

Undefeated boxer Floyd Mayweather has suggested to Spence Jr. to move up to weight classes

Spence Jr. lost to Crawford by a ninth-round technical knockout in Dallas, Texas, on July 29

'I need every fighter to know this: if you're with PBC, you're with Floyd Mayweather, period. OK? Period. You cannot bring up Al Haymon's name without bring up Floyd Mayweather's name. We're one. 

'With that being said, I want the best for Errol Spence,' added Mayweather. 'So, if I was training him, or I was in his corner, I don't think he should be fighting at 147 at all. I don't even think he should be fighting at 154.

'I think he should be fighting at 160 or 168. That's just my honest opinion. But, once again, I don't have no say, so. When these guys, certain fighters, when they want a fight, our job is to line it up. Us fighters we're warriors. We're always going to fight the best, that's just how it is.' 

Crawford said he'd be open to a re-match with Spence Jr later this year but he gets to decide which weight class they get to fight in, if that's the case

Ahead of his fight with Crawford, Spence suggested that his days at 147 were numbered. He can effectively activate his contractually mandated rematch clause with Crawford for a rematch to take place later this year. 

But, Crawford has the final say in the choice weight class. 'Bud' started his career as a lightweight and has won three titles across three divisions. 

And just like Spence Jr., Crawford has brought up the possibility that he could end up moving up to 154 for the rematch as well. 

Mayweather, whose last competitive fight was against Conor McGregor, has been involved in exhibitions for the last few years. 

His matchup with John Gotti III, in June, stemmed a post-fight brawl.
