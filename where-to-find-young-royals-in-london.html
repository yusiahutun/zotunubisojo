Young Royal's Favorite London Hang-outs

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, July 23, 2024

Before Prince William and Duchess Kate were married, they were faithful fixtures on the London nightlife scene, regularly spotted at Boujis on Tuesdays and Mahiki on Thursdays. After the wedding, the deluge of club visits trickled to a drop, and now that they're parents, you're more likely to spot them in the Kensington Palace Gardens than the loos at Maggie's. Luckily for royal watchers, their younger siblings and cousins have eagerly taken up the mantle. Prince Harry is rising to the challenge in his public life, just completing a royal tour of Nepal and helming the charity Sentebale– but he's showing no signs of settling down in private. He's often spotted around town knocking back drinks, frequently with his cousin Princess Eugenie of York.

Not surprisingly, you're most likely to find London's HRH's hanging out in establishments owned by long-time friends part of their tight-knit circle. This crew is all about privacy and discretion—although behind closed doors, they're famous for letting their hair down and letting the good times roll. If you're hoping to be besties with Bea or go Harry hunting, look no further—these are the spots where you're most likely to spot royals in London right now. Although, this being London, establishments enjoying royal favor are all-but-guaranteed to change in 3…2…1…

CHILTERN FIREHOUSE

This Andre Balazs-owned hotel/restaurant/bar, in a circa 1890's fire station in Marylebone, has been the place to go out ever since it opened in 2013. Normally, London hotspots have a short shelf-life–but the party at Chiltern shows no signs of slowing down. Guests have included everybody from David Cameron to Bill Clinton to Bono, and young royals in particular can't get enough. Princess Beatrice and Princess Eugenie are regularly photographed entering and exiting, often with Prince Harry's ex-girlfriend Cressida Bonas in tow. Chelsy Davy (another former Harry paramour), Pippa and James Middleton are fans, too. Inside, you'll find a restaurant helmed by Nuno Mendes, formerly of El Bulli, and a fairly hopping bar—but the real action takes place in the Ladder Shed, accessible only to hotel guests and those lucky enough to secure a special token.

Chiltern Firehouse; 1 Chiltern St, London W1U 7PA, London; ChilternFirehouse.com; +44 20 7073 7676

BUNGA BUNGA

Co-owned by Charlie Gilkes—an Old Etonian who used to date Pippa Middleton, and a friend of Harry and Eugenie's (both were guests at his wedding)–this is a kitschy, anything-goes Italian-themed pizza joint, inspired by former Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi. Although it's in Battersea—on the 'wrong' side of the Thames—it's perennially packed and turns into a full-on dance club at night, with cabaret acts, people dancing in the Eurovision Room, and karaoke in the back. Obviously, Hazza, Euge, Bea, and Pippa are regulars.

Bunga Bunga, 37 Battersea Bridge Road, London SW11 3BA;; 020 7095 0360

Johnny's Photography / Courtesy of Bunga Bunga

Italian fare at Bunga Bunga

LOULOU'S

For aristocratic Brits of a certain age—think Fergie and Di—the only place to be seen was legendary members-only club Annabel's. Loulou's is Annabel's spawn, freshly created by and for a new generation (it's owned by Robin Birley, the son of Annabel's proprietor Mark Birley, and named after his aunt, fashion designer Loulou de la Falaise). Dimly-lit and brimming with the whimsical décor beloved by English toffs—there's a stuffed giraffe by the bar—Loulou's is the type of well-connected, everybody-knows-everybody place that doesn't need or want your attention, thank you very much. On any given night, you're apt to find the usual set. Beatrice and Eugenie are often there with public school friends in tow and William and Kate have been known to make the rare appearance.

Loulou's, 2-5 Hertford St, London W1J 7RB;  +44 20 7408 2100

SOHO FARMHOUSE

An outpost of the Soho House group, this 100-acre countryside estate is a dreamy weekend retreat complete with stables, Cowshed spa, boathouse, and a winter ice-skating rink. Most of the property includes standalone cabins for added privacy, and the food truck and cocktail truck operate 24/7, allowing guests to request a bacon buttie or G&T at anytime, day or night. Prince Harry occasionally sneaks off here, and recently persuaded them to keep their Mill Bar pub open until 4 am (although Mark Ronson reportedly holds the 7:30 am partying record). Other fans include Princess Eugenie and her bestie Cressida Bonas, plus Alexa Chung, Eddie Redmayne and Liv Tyler.

Soho Farmhouse, 1 Tracey Farm Cottages, Great Tew, Chipping Norton OX7 4JS;  +44 1608 691000

THE BROWN COW

Owned by Prince Harry's close friend Mark Dyer, a former officer in the Welsh Guards, The Brown Cow pub's Fulham location makes it a favorite local watering hole for young Sloanes. (You can't swing a cat in this area without hitting the daughter of a Duchess or son of an Earl.) The seasonal menu is more posh than pub: one appetizer includes foie gras and chicken liver parfait with white wine jelly and toasted brioche. Still, the atmosphere is lively and convivial, making it a preferred stop for Prince Harry: this is where he celebrated Prince George's birth and had regular date nights with Cressida. Most tellingly, right after Harry returned from Afghanistan, The Brown Cow is the first place he headed to wet his whistle.

The Brown Cow, 676 Fulham Rd, London SW6 5SA, United Kingdom; +44 20 7384 9559

Courtesy of The Brown Cow

THE CROSS KEYS

Another Mark Dyer-owned property, The Cross Keys enjoys the distinction of being Chelsea's oldest pub—although it was boarded up for several years until Dyer bought and renovated it, turning it into a chic gastropub. Prince Harry spent his 31st birthday here with friends and family—including his cousin Zara Tindall, Princess Anne's daughter—and was seen sharing a cigarette outside.

The Cross Keys, 1 Lawrence St, London SW3 5NB; +44 207 351 0686

Courtesy of The Cross Keys

GEISHA AT RAMUSAKE

A "festive dining" bar inside the late-night members' only Japanese Izakaya restaurant Ramusake, Geisha is run by Guy Pelly, William and Harry's close friend since childhood. Pelly knows how to throw a party (he was in charge of William's bachelor party, and also created London's beloved, though now-defunct, Public), and so Geisha patrons like Prince Harry and Princess Beatrice are as likely to find themselves sipping on sake cocktails as they are staring down a fire-breathing sumo wrestler, DJs in Kabuki masks or sparkler-toting women in (nothing but) lacy underwear.

Ramusake, 92B Old Brompton Rd, London SW7 3LQ; +44 20 7842 8518

Instagram

THE GORING HOTEL

The Goring has a long and storied royal history– it's the only hotel with a royal warrant for hospitality, was a favorite spot of The Queen Mother and was recently redesigned by Princess Margaret's son David Lindley. The hotel kitchen baked Prince Charles' Christening cake, and was a favorite tea spot for Queen Mary. However, it's the next generation of royals who have kept it in public consciousness. Duchess Kate famously stayed here with family the night before the royal wedding, and she even made an appearance for The Goring's 105th anniversary party. Prince Harry has fallen off its terrace and partied here alongside Beatrice and Eugenie, and it's rumored that there is a secret tunnel connecting it to Buckingham Palace, which is just 'round the corner.

The Goring Hotel, Beeston Pl, London SW1W 0JW; +44 20 7396 9000

Courtesy of The Goring

THE ARTS CLUB

Founded in 1863 as a male-only literary, arts and science club—co-founders and early members included Charles Dickens, Anthony Trollope, and Rudyard Kipling—The Arts Club has become the chicest place in London. Prince Philip is the patron (his portrait hangs in a stairwell), and in addition to the Brasserie restaurant, bar, garden terrace and Club Nouveau nightclub, it also includes just 16 hotel rooms, only available to members and their guests. Royal fans include Prince Harry, Princess Beatrice, Princess Eugenie, and Princess Diana's niece Kitty Spencer, while Hollywood royalty loves it, too: Victoria and David Beckham and Beyonce and Jay-Z have been spotted there; and Gwyneth Paltrow is such a fan that she's reportedly planning to open an outpost in West Hollywood soon.

The Arts Club, 40 Dover St, London W1S 4NP; +44 20 7499 8581
