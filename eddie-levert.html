Eddie Levert – Career and Professional Life

His career started when he was still in high school. Eddie Levert formed a band named The Triumphs with his high school friends. His bandmates included Walter Willimas, William Powell, Bobby Massey, and Bill Isles.

The band renamed itself quite a few times before settling down with The O’Jays. Similarly, they first performed in Canton locally. When they traveled to Cincinnati to Canvas King Records, where the band was renamed ‘The Mascots’.

Under this name, their popularity started growing as Eddie and his band’s songs were broadcast on different radio stations.

Likewise, they later signed with Philadelphia International Records in 1969 and started to produce more music. They have produced many songs throughout their careers. Some of their hit songs are ‘Let Me Touch You’, ‘Lovin’ You’, and many more.

A few of their songs topped the charts as well. Eddie doesn’t limit himself just to the band but also has a solo career. He has released solo songs as well.
