David Shuster Bio | Wiki

David Shuster is an American veteran journalist working as the anchor and host of The Young Turks. Before joining the Young Turks in July 2021, he was working as an on-air contributor for Quick Hits in New York.

David Shuster Education

He is a 1995 graduate of Bloomington High School South. David later proceeded to the University of Michigan where he earned a bachelor of arts in Political Science in 1989. David Shuster MSNBC

David was also the lead breaking news anchor for MSNBC before he was suspended for an indefinite period. While working there, David interviewed former President Jimmy Carter, Congresswoman Marsha Blackburn, and also provided coverage for Hurricane Katrina and the death of Michael Jackson.

Additionally, he also traveled to Qatar to provide the coverage of USA Central Command during Operation Iraqi Freedom. Prior to working for MSNBC, David was the Washington D.C. correspondent for Fox News between 1996-2002. During his time there, he covered the 9/11 attacks, the Monica Lewinsky scandal, the Senate impeachment trial, and the Starr Report.

David had a kick start at his journalism career as a field producer and assignment editor for CNN based in Washington. However, he left CNN to join KATV in Arkansas as a political reporter where he provided coverage for the Whitewater scandal.
