Goldie Hawn gushes over controversial actor Mel Gibson

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, September 7, 2024

They're both heading Down Under to promote upcoming projects.

And legendary Hollywood actress Goldie Hawn, 70, has praised controversial actor Mel Gibson, 60, while also revealing the secrets to her career success.

During an interview with Today on Friday, the Oscar-winning actress described her Bird on a Wire co-star as a family friend who she insists is a lot of 'fun'.

The twice-married actress revealed her partner of more than 30 years Kurt Russell, along with her two children Kate and Oliver, are currently working on a TV series with father-of-eight Mel.

'He has come over to the house quite a bit... the family are all loving him,' she said.

Despite his success in Hollywood, Mel's career has been overshadowed by scandal, including controversial remarks about minority groups.

In 1991, he made derogatory comments about the LGBT community, and in 2006 and 2010 respectively he was criticised for making antisemitic and racist comments.

Almost 50 years after Goldie burst onto the acting scene in the 1968 film Giggly Girl, the actress - who went on to appear in blockbusters like Private Benjamin and The First Wives Club - revealed the secret to her success in Hollywood.

'I think I have a personally trait, one of them, and that is that I don't give up. Always keep your dreams alive and be happy. I think that is a big one,' she said. 

As her 71st birthday approaches, the acclaimed star is set to return to the Gold Coast next month, where she will host a talk about her career and life philosophy.

Hollywood royalty: Goldie starred in the 1996 comedy The First Wives Club

Goldie took a 14-year acting hiatus in 2002, she is set to return to the big screen next year.

The seasoned beauty is currently working on the upcoming film Mother/Daughter, written by comedic actress Amy Schumer.

The film also stars Wanda Sykes and Christopher Meloni and is set for release next year.
