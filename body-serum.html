Do you really need a body serum? The benefits of body serums

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, April 21, 2024

What’s your morning beauty routine? Shampoo and soap, followed by moisturiser? Or a series of scalp scrubs, body oils, serums, essences and primers, all layered, mixed and spritzed in order to prepare you for each day? Our bathroom cabinets might be more jam-packed than ever, but if you thought the beauty industry had already formulated a product for literally every hair and skin concern imaginable, you were wrong.

Introducing the latest category coming for your shelf space: body serums. That’s right, just as our facial skincare routines have evolved to incorporate serums promising to resurface, rejuvenate, refine and restore our complexions, bodycare is now taking its cues from those same face-grade ingredients and innovative formulations. Some, such as Nécessaire’s Body Serum and Susanne Kaufmann’s Hyaluron Body Gel, focus on hydration, looking to skincare hero hyaluronic acid to help restore the protective skin barrier and enable better moisture retention. Others contain powerful actives more commonly associated with facial skincare, designed to tackle issues like body acne and loss of firmness. Think Tata Harper’s new Resurfacing Body Serum, which features fruit-based vitamin C, exfoliating alpha hydroxy acids and plant-derived vitamin A, or Typology’s Purifying Body Serum, with zinc PCA and tea tree extract.

As yet, the body serum category remains small, but it’s growing. Data from market research company Mintel shows that 4 per cent of bodycare launches in the UK during 2021 were serums, up from 1 per cent in 2019, while 33 per cent of bodycare users said that they have layered products for maximum results. What happened to slapping a bit of body lotion onto your dry patches and calling it a day?

“Thanks to ingredient-focused brands like The Ordinary, and the rise of ‘skinfluencers’ on Instagram and TikTok, consumers are clued-up about skincare actives and recognise their power in treating skin concerns, on both body and face,” says Georgia Stafford, beauty analyst at Mintel, who predicts that demand will grow further still thanks to the buzz generated by these latest launches. “This skincare knowledge is helping to drive the adoption of multi-step bodycare routines that rely on active ingredients to deal with skin concerns like uneven tone or keratosis pilaris.”

Tata Harper introduced her eponymous brand’s body serum partly because of her own desire to expand her bodycare routine. She believes that if you’re looking for better results from your bodycare, your routine should mimic the one you use for your face.

“Your skin is your body’s largest organ, so why stop at the neck?” Harper says. “Just like facial serums, body serums deliver active ingredients to target specific concerns such as dry or dull skin, whereas traditional body lotions are mainly used to provide moisture. Serums are able to deliver a higher concentration of active ingredients than other lotions, creams and ointments. This small, additional step makes a major difference in the overall look and health of the skin, creating undeniable results.”

If all of this fills you with dread, and visions of even longer spent trying to get out of the door in the morning, then fear not. In terms of whether you really and truly need a body serum as well as a body lotion, if you’re happy with your current routine, the answer is probably no. Even Randi Christiansen, co-founder of Nécessaire, is clear, when I speak to her, that the brand’s Body Serum is only “necessary” if you have a currently unmet concern and are looking for something to address it, rather than merely adding yet another product to your daily tally. But if you feel your body needs just a little something more, the gel-like formula, which incorporates five molecular weights of hyaluronic acid, delivers an extra moisture boost to parched skin.

For some, a body serum might even replace a lotion, especially during summer. Nécessaire’s Body Serum and Susanne Kaufmann’s Hyaluron Body Gel both work really well as alternatives to a thicker body lotion, absorbing quickly and making great lightweight hydrators in hot weather. Both feel like the sort of thing you’d want to apply to sun-kissed skin on holiday, after a cool, early-evening shower. Meanwhile, Typology’s Purifying Body Serum is designed to be used only on areas affected by blemishes, offering a booster-like effect where needed, rather than requiring full-body application.

Or maybe you really don’t mind spending a little extra time on your bodycare routine. If that’s you, you’re far from alone. Whilst the market for body serums remains small, its growth in recent years taps into our collective desire to care for ourselves and our bodies, and to spend time nourishing them.

“While pre-pandemic lifestyles have largely returned, consumers discovered value in multi-step routines as a form of self-care when they had extra time during lockdowns,” reveals Stafford. “Bodycare now means more to consumers than quickly moisturising, as it can be a time to unwind and indulge.”

Just ask self-confessed “skincare maximalist” Tata Harper. “For my body skincare regimen, I begin by applying the Tata Harper Smoothing Body Scrub on dry skin, to buff and polish before rinsing in the shower. After drying off, I use the Resurfacing Body Serum to brighten, hydrate and smooth my skin, followed by the Revitalizing Body Oil to deeply nourish and lock in the benefits from the serum,” she explains. “Incorporating a body serum into your post-shower routine is an easy way to give your body the treatment it deserves, and keep your skin looking radiant from head-to-toe.”
