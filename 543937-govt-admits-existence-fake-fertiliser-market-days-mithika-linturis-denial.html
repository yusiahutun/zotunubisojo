Nairobi - The Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development has confirmed the presence of fake fertiliser in the Kenyan market.

In a statement on Friday, April 5, the Ministry stated that most of the fertiliser in circulation under the subsidy program was up to standard except one.

According to the officials at Kilimo House, the products branded Kelphos Plus, Kelphos gold, and NPK 10:26:10 did not meet the required parameters.

"The government has completed the testing on all fertilizers being distributed under subsidy programme and wish to confirm to the public and farmers that all fertilizers being distributed meet the required quality requirements except those manufactured and distributed by Ms KEL Chemicals branded Kelphos Plus, Kelphos gold and NPK 10:26:10, which did not meet all required test parameters. This consignment affected KEL Chemical fertilizers distributed between 5th and 10th March 2024," read the statement in part.

The government stated that the Kenya Bureau of Standard (KEBS had suspended KEL Chemicals' operations and seized its fertiliser products.

The Ministry added that they had impounded all fertiliser stocks distributed by KEL Chemicals at the National Cereals Produce Board (NCPB) facilities.

Farmers who purchased the sub-standard commodity have also been advised to stop using it and visit the nearest NCPB depot for further directions.

"The Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development strongly advises farmers who have acquired or possess fertiliser from KEL Chemicals to immediately discontinue its use and to visit their nearest NCPB facility for further guidance," continued the statement.

They stated that they are committed to enforcing strict compliance with safety and quality standards for all fertilisers used by Kenyan farmers.

Linturi blames Azimio for spread of fake fertiliser

Earlier, Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Mithika Linturi urged farmers to ignore reports about fake fertiliser.

Linturi accused opposition leaders of spreading false information to sabotage President William Ruto's agenda.

He said Azimio leaders were demonstrating on the streets instead of listening to the president's advice about farming.
