Kalonzo Musyoka Claims Charity Ngilu Opposed His Reunion with Raila: &quot;She Incited Him&quot;

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, August 26, 2024
  • Kalonzo Musyoka gave a glimpse into the intrigues leading up to his endorsement of ODM leader Raila Odinga as the Azimio-OKA coalition presidential aspirant
  • Kalonzo regretted that some leaders around Raila were opposed to him and other One Kenya Alliance principals signing a political pact with the former premier
  • The politician claimed that Charity Ngilu was one of the leaders who had obstructed and incited Raila against signing the agreement with him

Wiper Party leader Kalonzo Musyoka has accused Kitui governor Charity Ngilu of frustrating his efforts to reunite with ODM leader Raila Odinga.

Kalonzo claimed Ngilu had obstructed and incited Raila against signing the agreement with him.

This came a few days after the politician threw his weight behind the Azimio La Umoja leader.

Kalonzo, who was speaking at his Tseikuru home in Kitui County on Sunday, March 13, said Raila had refused to sign an agreement with him because of Ngilu.

“I was reading the papers saying that in the six hours I was refusing to do... I-don’t-know-what. The truth is that Raila had refused to sign, and he was incited by Ngilu so that Kalonzo does not benefit from anything,” said Kalonzo.“They did not want Raila to sign anything with Azimio-OKA in it. At the last minute, Raila emerged with Ngilu and I was accompanied by Gideon Moi, that is the whole truth,” he added.

According to Kalonzo, Odinga had the moral obligation to support him in the August polls because he supported him in the 2017 polls.

“Some were angry when I said ‘Raila tosha’, and as the bishop told us this morning, do our enemy good… What I said at Jacaranda, if Raila has a good heart, he should have said my brother Stephen tosha. But he was incited by the likes of Ngilu not to agree to that. We must tell the truth,” Kalonzo added.

The veteran politician further assured Kitui residents that all stalled projects in the area will be completed.

“When Uhuru comes here I want that office to be modified. It was never renovated because Kalonzo was in opposition, I am going to tell the CS and PS Kibicho, now that there is no difference between Uhuru and Kalonzo, to send people to come and complete this work,” Kalozo said.

In shelving his presidential ambitions to back Odinga’s State House bid at Azimio La Umoja’s NDC on Saturday, March 12, Kalonzo who is aged 68, said ​​he considered the age factor between him and Raila, 77.

Kalonzo led his party in signing the Azimio coalition deal, which approved Raila as the ultimate presidential candidate.

"I have a political history with Raila. We have had a long journey together. We have braved teargas together, we started off at LDP with the likes of Andrew Ligale. We later came to Narc, transitioned to ODM and later CORD. We again united in NASA. I am today declaring my support for Raila for the third time," said Kalonzo.
