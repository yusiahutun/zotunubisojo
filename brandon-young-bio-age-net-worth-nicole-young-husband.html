Brandon Young Job

Brandon Young is the Director of Music Affairs at Activision. He’s been working with the video game company ever since August 2002.

So, over his 2 decades with the company. Brandon has led the musical creative direction for all of Activision’s in-game music initiatives, any/all music in marketing and promotional assets and partnerships, and, anything that touches music. He even led the music selection and negotiations on the famed Tony Hawk franchise in the early 2000s and has since placed and licensed tens of thousands of commercial songs in Activision games and promotional assets including some of the most iconic music collaborations in history, from Jay-Z to Taylor Swift.

Furthermore, Brandon has executive produced the scores behind the gaming industry’s all-time biggest-selling franchise Call of Duty since its origin — a game that consecutively broke its own records as the most successful entertainment launch in history.

Also, he was responsible for overseeing the music selection and in-game celebrity talent integrations for Activision’s acclaimed franchise, Guitar Hero, opening the door to work with music legends like Metallica, Aerosmith, Van Halen, and many other high-caliber talent partnerships. On top of that, he brought in legendary film composers with the likes of Grammy and Academy award winners Hans Zimmer, Bear McCreary, Jack Wall, Sarah Schachner, Harry Gregson-Williams, David Buckley, Graeme Revell, Lorne Balfe, and so many other legendary composers. 

Besides the above-mentioned games, Activision as a parent company operates massive subsidiary companies such as World of Warcraft, Overwatch, Diablo, StarCraft, Hearthstone, Candy Crush, Farmville, Crash Bandicoot, and Spyro and is a pioneer in franchising gaming leagues in the e-sports sector.

As for Brandon’s education, he graduated from Alan C. Pope High School and later got his Bachelor of Business Administration in Business Administration and Management from Georgia State University in May 1998.
