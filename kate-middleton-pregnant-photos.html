Princess of Wales Baby Bump Pictures

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, May 26, 2024

Several years and three kids later, Kate Middleton’s pregnancies still stand out as some of the royal family’s most celebrated moments. Up ahead, we’ll take a walk down memory lane with Kate Middleton’s pregnant photos, but first, let’s get to know the not-so-little-anymore kids of Cambridge.

Kate Middleton’s journey to motherhood began in 2012. After months of pregnancy rumors, the royal palace formally announced in December 2012 that she and her husband, Prince William, were expecting their first child. Kate, formally known as the Duchess of Cambridge at the time, didn’t have the easiest pregnancy—she was diagnosed with Hyperemesis Gravidarum, a severe form of morning sickness. “I was not the happiest pregnant person,” she later revealed in an episode of the Happy Mum, Happy Baby podcast in February 2020. “I was really sick, I wasn’t eating the things I should be eating.”

Despite her difficult pregnancy, Kate went on to welcome a healthy baby boy, making her and Prince William parents for the very first time. Their son, Prince George of Cambridge, was born on July 22, 2013 and is currently heir to the British throne in the line of succession after his father, Prince William. The future monarch was born at St. Mary’s Hospital in London—the same hospital where Prince William’s mother, Princess Diana, gave birth to him and his little brother, Prince Harry. Just hours after giving birth to their first child, Kate and William posed for photos outside of the hospital to give the public a glimpse at their newborn.

In September 2014, the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge announced they were expecting another child. Unfortunately, just as Kate struggled with severe morning sickness during her first pregnancy, this time was no different. “We’re immensely thrilled, it’s great news, early days but I’m hoping that things settle down [and] she feels a bit better,” Prince William told reporters at the start of Kate’s second pregnancy, per CNBC. After being treated by doctors at home at Kensington Palace throughout her pregnancy, Kate went on to welcome a baby girl named Charlotte Elizabeth Diana on May 2, 2015. Just like her older brother George, William and Kate’s second child was introduced to the world outside of St. Mary’s hospital.

Charlotte’s name is an homage to her royal family members. Her first name is the feminine version of her grandfather Prince Charles’ name. Meanwhile, her middle names pay tribute to her late grandmother, Princess Diana, and her great-grandmother, Queen Elizabeth. Before her great-granddaughter was born, Queen Elizabeth introduced the Succession to the Crown Act of 2013, which now allowed both sons and daughters of any future monarch to have an equal right to the throne (previously, only male heirs had a right to succession.) This means Charlotte—whose official title is Her Royal Highness Princess Charlotte of Cambridge—is officially third in the line of succession to the British throne after her older brother, George, and her father, Prince William.

“It’s very special having a new little girl. I feel very, very lucky that George has got a little sister,” Kate shared at the time. “The Queen was really thrilled that it was a little girl, and I think as soon as we came back here to Kensington she was one of our first visitors here.”

Just over two years after the birth of their daughter, William and Kate announced they were expecting their third child. The couple welcomed their son, Louis Arthur Charles, on April 23, 2018. Like his older siblings, his name has a sentimental meaning to the family. It has been speculated that he was named after his great-grandfather Prince Philip’s own grandfather, Prince Louis Alexander of Battenberg, along with Prince Charles’ great-uncle and mentor, Earl Louis Mountbatten.

Officially speaking, Louis is known as His Royal Highness Prince Louis of Cambridge and holds the fourth position in the line of succession to the British throne behind his older siblings and his father, Prince William. Despite being the youngest of the bunch, Louis has arguably sparked the most attention out of his siblings thanks to his “cheeky” personality, having made headlines for his silly faces and antics at royal events throughout the years.

Kate Middleton Pregnant Photos

Now that we know who’s who in the Cambridge brood, it’s time to take a look at Kate Middleton’s pregnancy photos to see Prince George, Princess Charlotte, and Prince Louis back when they were still inside their mother’s tummy. Keep scrolling ahead for Kate Middleton’s pregnant photos throughout the years.

    Prince William and Kate Middleton were photographed leaving King Edward VII hospital in London, England early on in Kate’s first pregnancy. The Duchess of Cambridge was being treated at the hospital for her acute morning sickness symptoms.

  • February 19, 2013: Kate Middleton at Hope House

    When the Duchess of Cambridge was around four months pregnant with Prince George, she paid a visit to Hope House, an all-female rehabilitation center and one of the projects run by her patronage, Action on Addiction.

  • March 5, 2013: Kate Middleton in Grimsby, England

    While pregnant with Prince George, Kate paid a visit to the National Fishing Heritage Centre during a day of public engagements in Grimsby, England.

    The Duchess of Cambridge was around five months pregnant with Prince George when she attended the St Patrick’s Day Parade at Mons Barracks alongside her husband, Prince William.

  • April 4, 2013: Kate Middleton in Glasgow, Scotland

    Kate Middleton, joined by her husband Prince William, visited the Donald Dewar Leisure Centre to launch a new project for The Royal Foundation of The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge in Glasgow, Scotland while pregnant with Prince George.

  • April 21, 2013: Kate Middleton at Windsor Castle

    The Duchess of Cambridge attended the National Review of Queen’s Scouts at Windsor Castle, pictured here, during her pregnancy with Prince George.

    At six months pregnant, the Duchess of Cambridge attended the inauguration of Warner Bros. newest studio in Leavesden in Watford, Hertfordshire, in the East of England.

  • May 22, 2013: Kate Middleton at Buckingham Palace Garden Party

    Kate Middleton attended a garden party hosted by Queen Elizabeth II on Buckingham Palace grounds when she was seven months pregnant with Prince George.

    In her last official engagement before Prince George was born, Kate Middleton attended Queen Elizabeth’s annual Trooping the Colour Ceremony. Here, she is pictured on the balcony of Buckingham Palace, where she was joined by her brother-in-law, Prince Harry, and her husband, Prince William.

  • December 9, 2014: Kate Middleton in New York City

    Kate Middleton was around five months pregnant with Princess Charlotte when she attended the St. Andrews 600th Anniversary Dinner on December 9, 2014 in New York City with her husband Prince William.

  • January 15, 2015: Kate Middleton at Barlby Primary School

    As a Royal Patron, Kate visited Barlby Primary School in London, England to bestow an official name to the school’s art room while pregnant with Princess Charlotte.

  • January 19, 2015: Kate Middleton at Kensington Leisure Centre

    During her pregnancy with Princess Charlotte, the Duchess of Cambridge had the honor of formally opening the Kensington Leisure Centre in London, England, where she is pictured here.

  • February 12, 2015: Kate Middleton in Portsmouth, England

    Around seven months into her pregnancy with Princess Charlotte, Kate Middleton stopped in Portsmouth, England to visit the new home of Ben Ainslie Racing and the 1851 Trust to view an art project by the local community.

    During a visit to Stoke on Trent, the Duchess of Cambridge shopped at the Emma Bridgewater Factory while pregnant with Princess Charlotte.

  • March 9, 2015: Kate Middleton at Commonwealth Observance Ceremony

    Kate Middleton attended the Commonwealth Observance Service at Westminster Abbey when she was about eight months pregnant with Princess Charlotte. Her husband, Prince William, was also by her side.

  • March 13, 2015: Kate Middleton at Afghanistan Troops Commemoration Service

    Prince William and Kate Middleton attended a Service of Commemoration for troops who were stationed in Afghanistan in London when the Duchess of Cambridge was eight months pregnant with Princess Charlotte.

  • January 17, 2018: Kate Middleton visits Great Ormond Street Hospital

    The Duchess of Cambridge paid a visit to Great Ormond Street Hospital in London, England, when she was six months pregnant with Prince Louis. She met with families and spent time playing with young patients at the hospital’s opening event.

  • January 31, 2018: Kate Middleton in Stockholm, Sweden

  • February 1, 2018: Kate Middleton in Oslo, Norway

    At eight months pregnant with Prince Louis, the Duchess of Cambridge attended a symposium at the Royal Society of Medicine about early mental health intervention for children and families. This marked one of her last official engagements before giving birth her youngest son.

  • April 1, 2018: Kate Middleton at St. George's Chapel

    For her last appearance before giving birth to Prince Louis, the Duchess of Cambridge attended Easter Service at St George’s Chapel in Windsor, England with the rest of the royal family.
