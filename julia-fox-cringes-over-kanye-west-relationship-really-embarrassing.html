Julia Fox 'cringes' over Kanye West relationship - 'Really embarrassing'

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, August 10, 2024

Julia Fox and Kanye West’s brief relationship seems like a distant memory, but it seems like the model hasn’t forgotten, as she seemingly calls the star ’embarrassing’.

The former celebrity couple began dating in January 2022, one year after Kim Kardashian and Kanye West called time on their marriage. However, they called it quits less than two months later, and the Hollywood star hasn’t stayed quiet about the relationship, even mentioning parts in her memoir, Down the Drain.

Julia Fox seemingly calls Kanye West relationship ’embarrassing’

The Uncut Gems actress was asked which of her exes are the “most cringe,” in a game of cheap shots, and it looked like she didn’t want to take a shot.

“All of them guys, they’re all really cringe,” she told Cosmopolitan. “They’re all really embarrassing. From my baby daddy to the other one,” seemingly speaking about Ye.

However, the Runaway rapper was beaten to the title of “most cringe” as that went to Julia’s “drug dealer boyfriend” who she claimed ‘kidnapped her when she was in high school and didn’t let her leave his house’.

Fox also referred to her ex-husband, pilot Peter Artemiev in the video. The couple were married for two years before calling it quits in 2020. In 2021, when the relationship was over, they welcomed their son, Valentino, a year before getting with West.

Ye, who is now married to Bianca Censori, was going through a divorce at the time of the relationship, as his separation from Kim Kardashian wasn’t finalized until November 2022.

Although a ‘brief fling’ Fox detailed her relationship with Ye in her memoir, claiming he offered to get her a boob job and “pressured her” to sign an NDA.

The star would ‘never date another celebrity publically’

Earlier this week, the movie star revealed she was “scarred” from her relationship with Ye, and that she wouldn’t date another celebrity publically again.

“What I find happens so often in public relationships – at least what happened to me, and maybe I’m just scarred from it – is that you become known as that person’s girlfriend,” the 34-year-old told Stellar magazine.

“And I just don’t want to uphold that. I want to be able to make it on my own. I don’t want anyone to be able to attribute my success to a man in my life,” she continued. “If anything, men are an obstacle I have to get through. It has never helped me.”
