Getting ready with Bridgerton's India Amarteifio and Michael Kors

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, September 15, 2024

India Amarteifio is one of the new faces joining the Bridgerton world – and is also fast making a name for herself in the fashion world, too. The 21-year-old actress plays young Queen Charlotte in the new spin-off show, Queen Charlotte: A Bridgerton Story, coming to Netflix in May.

While we still have a few weeks to wait until we see Amarteifio on our screens, that doesn't mean the rising star has a quieter schedule. We spoke to her ahead of a Netflix panel talk in LA she was participating in – part of the promo tour for the upcoming spin-off – where she dressed in an elegant red Michael Kors Collection bodysuit and palazzo pants, from the brand's spring/summer 2023 collection.

Paige Campbell Linden

When it comes to her own personal style ethos, the actress keeps it simple: confidence and comfort are key. "If I feel confident in an outfit, it's probably going to look much better when I'm wearing it," she explains. "You can tell a mile off when someone has been dressed in something that they're not comfortable in. Comfort over everything."

Below, Amarteifio talks to Bazaar about the styling process, becoming a more conscious shopper and why what we wear matters.

Talk us through your look for today's event – how did you decide on it?

"I'm wearing Michael Kors – both myself and my stylist Holly White wanted something that was quite vibrant and empowering to go with such an event. I love when women go bold with their colour choices, so I wanted to do something similar."

Paige Campbell Linden

What makes the brand such a great fit for your style?

"I love class and sophistication when it comes to style, and Michael Kors does just that. The brand is known for having an elevated office look, which is something I'm really interested in emulating. I love feeling smart yet casual and looking like a million bucks, which is exactly how I feel wearing this outfit. It's very rare that off the models and away from the runway, the outfits look just as good without [you needing to be] being 6 foot something."

How involved are you with the styling process?

"I'm very involved. I tend to send my stylist ideas of what I'm wanting to achieve for a particular event, and then we do fittings for a couple of hours. We discuss everything there, even down to where the cuff sits on the wrist. I think this really comes across when people wear clothes that they feel genuinely happy and themselves in – you can see that added boost of confidence."

Paige Campbell Linden

Paige Campbell Linden

What kind of outfits make you feel your most confident?

"I love a good suit. I like anything androgynous-looking and quite boxy, because then it's not about my body; instead, it's about how I'm wearing the outfit and me feeling cool and confident. On the flip side, I also love bodycon, which can be very sophisticated and chic."

How has your style evolved over the years?

"As I've got older, I'm not looking to stand out quite as much as when I was growing up, where I'd always go for more quirky things. I've definitely toned it down and I'm into the Scandinavian way of dressing and keeping things simple; I like layering and rely on a few essential items in my wardrobe. As sustainability is becoming more prevalent in how we need to move forward in society, I've been shopping more consciously by buying one outfit I can re-wear instead of five new ones. That's part of what makes fashion so fun: you can take one thing and wear it in lots of different ways."

Paige Campbell Linden

How has your confidence developed on the red carpet?

"I put this down to my stylist. She always makes me feel so empowered when I work with her and always ensures that there is choice, as something might look great on me but I might not feel confident in it. She's never going to push me to wear anything [that I don't feel comfortable in] or that doesn't match my idea of how I want to look. She makes me feel confident in whatever I'm wearing."

What is your ideal getting-ready set up?

"Very quiet and chill, perhaps in a nice hotel which is basically what I'm doing now in Los Angeles with the sun out and music on. A lot of these events are filled with lots of people and are quite hectic, so I love feeling zen."

Paige Campbell Linden

Whose style have you always admired and why?

"Zoë Kravitz, which is probably down to her amazing parents. She's very cool and rocker with lots of monochromatic colours which I love – she makes everything look good and is one of those people who could wear a bin bag and still turn heads. Also the Olsen twins – I love their brand The Row, it's so understated, chic and very cool."

Paige Campbell Linden

What’s the best piece of fashion advice you’ve ever been given?

"What you wear is how you feel. If I feel confident in an outfit, it's probably going to look much better when I'm wearing it. You can tell a mile off when someone has been dressed in something that they're not comfortable in. Comfort over everything."

Fashion has a reputation for being frivolous, but why does what we wear matter?

"It's a physical representation of you and your soul as a person. It's a luxury to be able to put on outfits that reflect how we are and express how we want to be perceived by the world. If I'm not feeling confident one day, but I put on some really chunky Dr Martens and a boxy jumper, I look like I know what I'm doing. It's a way of comforting that inner soul. It's so important and says so much about us."

Paige Campbell Linden
