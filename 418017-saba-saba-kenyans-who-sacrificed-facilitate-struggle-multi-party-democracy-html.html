Kenya is today Wednesday, July 7, marking the 31st anniversary of Saba Saba, a day when protesters in the country petitioned for multiparty democracy in 1990.

For three decades now, Kenyans have always marked the day with several activities among them pushing for better living conditions from the government.

Riots reported across the country

On July 7, 1990, extensive riots were reported in the capital Nairobi and other towns during the first week of the month as rowdy opposition politicians led by Kenneth Matiba, Charles Rubia and Raila Odinga among others struggled for freedom to end KANU's one-party rule.

The riots which started in June 1990 and peaked in July were sparked by the then late president Daniel arap Moi's move to silence political opposition and mounting criticism against corruption and refusal to restore Kenya to a multiparty democracy.

A pro-democracy rally organised by members of opposition groups at Kamukunji grounds in Nairobi, on July 4, 1990, was declared illegal.

More than 6,000 people reportedly turned up and riots subsequently broke out as police officers dispersed the protestors forcefully using batons and teargas.

Famous Saba Saba day

The rally culminated into the famous Saba Saba day leading to Kenya's struggle for the second liberation.

The riots reportedly lasted four days and left 20 people dead, many others injured and more than 1,000 in Jail.

Rubia, Raila and Matiba were among those detained without trial as Moi moved to silence dissidents.

Other leaders who were either arrested or put under house arrest were Jaramogi Odinga, Gitobu Imanyara, Mohammed Ibrahim, Masinde Muliro, Martin Shikuku and James Orengo.

Push for new reforms

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) national devolution secretary Kipkorir Menjo, one of the opposition politicians on the frontline in pushing for new reforms to allow multiparty democracy which Moi had banned following the failed 1982 coup said that the country had achieved a lot to this extend.

''Today, we have a lot of freedom as Kenyans which is enshrined in the Constitution. People express their opinions freely without the fear of getting arrested unlike during the dark days of the brutal KANU era,'' Menjo told TUKO.co.ke in an interview.

Menjo who hails from Uasin Gishu county however expressed concern about the runaway corruption in government institutions that still continues to negatively affect the socio-economic fabric of the country.

''In my own view, I feel that the looting of government resources is the biggest concern at the moment. Unfortunately, those who loot don't seem to be scared. If the powers that be move with speed and tighten governance then we shall be on the right track,'' Menjo explained.

He mentioned the late Alexander Kipsang Muge was an Anglican bishop in Kenya, university students; Tirop Kitur, Kibor Talai and late human rights activist Ken Wafula as some of those he was in the second liberation struggle with.

Menjo also said that there is a need for the country's institutions to be effective.

''As we continue with this struggle for liberation, we should not get tired when it comes to holding them accountable,'' he said.

Former Mukurweini MP Kabando Wa Kabando who served as chairman of the Students Organization of Nairobi University (SONU) in 1992 said that to know what was happening in the country on the Saba Saba day in 1990, they had to listen to VoA, BBC or Radio Dutscheland in low volumes while in hiding.

''Patriots were killed, injured, detained, livelihoods destroyed in the fight for freedom. Politicians, journalists, students, lecturers, clergy, hawkers. We read anything critical of the KANU regime in hiding, then stuff it inside the mattresses. Times were really tough. Never again should we allow dictatorship of whatever form or aim. Freedom of speech, of assembly, of thought, of association inherent and inalienable,'' said Kabando in a series of tweets seen by TUKO.co.ke.

He said that 2021 is a critical year ushering in the 2022 General Election.

''This is a day to reflect and embolden. We must resist attempts to reintroduce political heroworship or cultism. 2021 we stare at serious threats to freedoms. We must resist,'' he added.

Dandora Community Justice Center leader Wilfred Olal was one of those arrested outside the National Archives, Nairobi and taken to Central Police station.

Olal was among other human rights defenders who had marched to the city's Central Business District (CBD) during the historical Saba Saba match.

Launch of Kenya's fourth Liberation Movement

The Communist Party of Kenya, The United Green Movement Party, Kenya Tuitakayo Movement and the National Students Caucus marked the day by launching Kenya's fourth Liberation Movement.

''When the liberation is finished, Kenya will establish inclusive institutions of an independent judiciary, a checked presidency, a parliament that is not suffocated, a robust civil society, a free and fair media, and a country where the people are sovereign enjoying life, liberty and pursuing happiness,'' the group said in a joint statement.

As the group launched the movement, it also promised to march to get rid of all individuals implicated in the country's mega scandals.

''We also want to get rid of these imperialists, these Anglo-leasers, these COVID-19 billionaires, these Arror-Kimwarer billionaires, these standard gauge railway profiteers. We will get rid of them. Perhaps it will cost us our life, but we are here to take risks. We are here to dare. We will push and move the struggle at all costs. We say to them; start trembling,'' the statement reads in part.

The group vowed to take the march to all parts of the country in the next few days until it reaches the journey of the fourth and final Liberation of Kenya.

''Which is that country that was envisaged by our founding forefathers; an inclusive country with plenty found within our borders,'' the organisations said.

As more and more Kenyans continue to call upon the government of the day to embrace prudent management of public affairs, it is a matter of wait and see if the fruits of Saba Saba day will be achieved.
