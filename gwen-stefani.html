Early Life

Gwen Renée Stefani was born on October 3, 1969. Her hometown is Fullerton, California. She was named after a character in the 1968 novel Airport. Similarly, her middle name, Renée, comes from the Four Tops’ 1968 rendition of the Left Banke’s 1966 song “Walk Away Renée.”

Dennis Stefani, an Italian-American, is her father’s name. Dennis Stefani worked as a marketing executive at Yamaha. Patti Flynn, Gwen’s mother, is an Irish American who worked as an accountant before becoming a homemaker. Stefani’s parents were folk music lovers. Her desire to become a vocalist began early in her childhood when she was exposed to the music of famous artists such as Bob Dylan and Emmylou Harris.

Stefani was raised as a Catholic in neighboring Anaheim, California, together with her three siblings. Jill and Todd are her younger siblings, while Eric is her elder brother. Eric was the keyboardist for No Doubt. Later he left the band to work as an animator on The Simpsons.
