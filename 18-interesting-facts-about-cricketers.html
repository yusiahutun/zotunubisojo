Sachin Tendulkar holds the record for the most runs scored in international cricket.

Regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket, Sachin Tendulkar's remarkable career spanned over two decades. The legendary cricketer amassed a staggering 34,357 runs across all formats of international cricket, setting a benchmark that may never be surpassed. His extraordinary consistency and sheer mastery of the game earned him the title of "Master Blaster" and solidified his status as an icon in the world of cricket.

Muttiah Muralitharan is the highest wicket-taker in international cricket.

Renowned for his mesmerizing spin bowling, Muttiah Muralitharan's unparalleled talent saw him claim a record-breaking 1,347 wickets in international cricket. The Sri Lankan maestro's ability to bamboozle batsmen with his unorthodox bowling action and unparalleled spin variations firmly established him as a bowling legend. His remarkable feat continues to inspire aspiring spin bowlers around the globe.

Sir Don Bradman's Test batting average remains unmatched.

Widely regarded as the greatest batsman in the history of cricket, Sir Don Bradman's extraordinary Test batting average of 99.94 stands as a testament to his unparalleled skill and dominance on the cricket field. The Australian maestro's exceptional consistency and ability to score runs against all oppositions cemented his legacy as an immortal figure in the sport.

Brian Lara holds the record for the highest individual score in first-class cricket.

Trinidadian batting sensation Brian Lara etched his name in cricketing folklore by registering an astonishing 501 not out in a first-class match, a record that still stands today. Lara's sublime strokeplay and unwavering focus propelled him to this extraordinary achievement, solidifying his status as one of the most prolific run-scorers in the history of the game.

Anil Kumble is the only bowler to have taken all ten wickets in a Test innings.

Indian spin maestro Anil Kumble etched his name in cricketing history by achieving the rare feat of claiming all ten wickets in a Test innings, a remarkable accomplishment that showcased his unparalleled skill and tenacity as a bowler. Kumble's unwavering determination and ability to outfox batsmen with his cunning spin bowling made him a formidable force in the world of cricket.

The highest individual score in One Day International (ODI) cricket is held by Rohit Sharma.

Indian batting sensation Rohit Sharma's scintillating knock of 264 runs in an ODI match against Sri Lanka remains the highest individual score in the history of the format. Known for his elegant strokeplay and ability to accelerate the scoring rate, Sharma's record-breaking innings showcased his exceptional talent and cemented his status as a modern-day batting maestro.

Women's cricket legend Mithali Raj holds the record for the most runs in One Day International (ODI) cricket.

Mithali Raj's prolific run-scoring prowess and unwavering consistency have propelled her to the summit of women's cricket, where she holds the record for the highest run aggregate in ODI cricket. The Indian batting stalwart's remarkable achievements have paved the way for future generations of female cricketers and solidified her status as an icon in the sport.

Sir Garfield Sobers achieved a historic feat by hitting six sixes in an over.

In a breathtaking display of power-hitting, West Indian all-rounder Sir Garfield Sobers etched his name in cricketing folklore by smashing six consecutive sixes in a single over, a feat that captivated the cricketing world and showcased his extraordinary batting prowess. Sobers' remarkable display of dominance and audacious strokeplay remains etched in the annals of cricket history.

Ellyse Perry is the first cricketer to represent Australia in both cricket and soccer World Cups.

Renowned for her exceptional sporting versatility, Ellyse Perry made history by representing Australia in both cricket and soccer World Cups, showcasing her remarkable talent and dedication across multiple sporting disciplines. Perry's extraordinary achievements have elevated her to legendary status in the world of sports, inspiring aspiring athletes to pursue excellence across diverse sporting arenas.

Wasim Akram is the only bowler to have taken two hat-tricks in consecutive Test matches.

Pakistani pace maestro Wasim Akram achieved a remarkable feat by claiming two hat-tricks in consecutive Test matches, a rare accomplishment that underscored his lethal bowling prowess and ability to dismantle opposition batting line-ups with unparalleled skill and precision. Akram's extraordinary talent and mastery of swing bowling solidified his status as a bowling icon in the history of cricket.

The highest individual score in Test cricket is held by Brian Lara.

Trinidadian batting maestro Brian Lara's monumental innings of 400 not out in a Test match against England remains the highest individual score in the history of the format. Lara's extraordinary feat showcased his unparalleled batting prowess and unwavering focus, etching his name in cricketing history as one of the most prolific run-scorers of all time.

Jhulan Goswami is the leading wicket-taker in Women's One Day International (ODI) cricket.

Indian pace sensation Jhulan Goswami's remarkable ability to consistently claim wickets has propelled her to the summit of women's ODI cricket, where she holds the record for the highest number of dismissals. Goswami's exceptional skill and dedication to her craft have established her as a trailblazer in women's cricket, inspiring future generations of fast bowlers to emulate her success.

Sir Vivian Richards scored the fastest century in Test cricket history.

West Indian batting icon Sir Vivian Richards blazed his way into the record books by notching the fastest century in Test cricket, a breathtaking innings that showcased his explosive batting prowess and unparalleled aggression at the crease. Richards' extraordinary feat remains etched in cricketing folklore as a testament to his indomitable spirit and fearless approach to batting.

Charlotte Edwards is the youngest cricketer to score 1,000 runs in Women's One Day International (ODI) cricket.

English batting prodigy Charlotte Edwards announced her arrival on the international stage by becoming the youngest cricketer to reach the milestone of 1,000 runs in women's ODI cricket, a remarkable achievement that highlighted her precocious talent and unwavering composure at the crease. Edwards' record-breaking feat set the stage for a stellar career in women's cricket, inspiring young players to strive for excellence.

Kapil Dev led India to its first-ever Cricket World Cup triumph.

Legendary all-rounder Kapil Dev captained the Indian cricket team to a historic victory in the 1983 Cricket World Cup, leading his side to their maiden triumph in the prestigious tournament. Dev's inspirational leadership and exceptional all-round contributions solidified his legacy as a pioneering figure in Indian cricket, sparking a new era of success and igniting the nation's passion for the sport.

Shoaib Akhtar holds the record for the fastest delivery bowled in international cricket.

Renowned as the "Rawalpindi Express," Pakistani speedster Shoaib Akhtar unleashed a thunderous delivery clocked at 161.3 km/h, setting the record for the fastest ball bowled in the history of international cricket. Akhtar's blistering pace and intimidating bowling style made him a formidable force on the cricket field, striking fear into the hearts of batsmen around the world.

Meg Lanning holds the record for the highest individual score in Women's One Day International (ODI) cricket.

Australian batting sensation Meg Lanning etched her name in the record books by smashing an exhilarating innings of 152 not out in a women's ODI match, setting the benchmark for the highest individual score in the history of the format. Lanning's remarkable achievement showcased her exceptional batting prowess and unwavering resolve, establishing her as a trailblazer in women's cricket.

Glenn McGrath holds the record for the most wickets in Cricket World Cup history.

Australian pace ace Glenn McGrath's phenomenal performances in the Cricket World Cup saw him claim a record 71 wickets in the prestigious tournament, solidifying his status as the leading wicket-taker in the history of the event. McGrath's remarkable consistency and ability to deliver breakthroughs on the grandest stage of cricket elevated him to legendary status in the annals of the sport.
