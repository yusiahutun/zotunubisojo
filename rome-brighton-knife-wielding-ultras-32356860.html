Inside notorious Roma ultras who are modelled on terror group with ambush-style attacks

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, February 29, 2024

Hooliganism is largely a thing of the past on these shores.

Seldom do you see mass fights break out between rival sets of fans intent on hurting each other. The same, however, can't be said for football on the continent.

The ugly side of the beautiful game reared its head again when Andy Le Goubin spent a night in hospital in Rome after he was slashed in the buttocks after following Brighton abroad. Andy, 26, was stabbed in streets before he was robbed of all his possessions. Although it is unclear if it was the work of the Roma ultras.

There have been long line of attacks from a notorious Roman ultra's group - Fedayn. Their name translates to 'The Devotees' and they're chillingly modelled after an Iranian terror group, using the same style of ambush attacks.

Hiding masks to conceal their identities, the group are known to wield knives - which are used to stab rival fans in the behind and the legs, to avoid a murder charge.

Geoff Pearson, a football violence researcher, told The Sun: "Fans of English sides are frequently targets of attacks by ultra groups abroad.

"Roma ultras, especially Fedayns, are quite active with atmosphere generation but some elements can also lead to them becoming involved in serious violent activity...Rome is certainly up there as one of the most dangerous cities a football fan can travel to."

Fedayns are normally quite easy to spot - they all wear the same uniform which features a thick belt. When needed, they will take this off and use it as a weapon to smack and whip fans into submission.

Alongside the belt, ultras are often seen dressed in a black hooded jacket, blue jeans and white trainers.

British fans might be familiar with Fedayns - in 2018, the group caused life-changing injuries to Liverpool fan Sean Cox. The unprovoked attack before a Champions League match left Cox with a brain injury as well as wheelchair-bound.

Simone Mastrelli, the hooligan responsible for the attack, was jailed. The judge said: "You must have picked him [Sean Cox] out because of the Liverpool scarf.

"Your head and face were covered by a hood and a balaclava and you were in front of Mr Cox with both arms raised towards him in a somewhat aggressive or confrontational way."

In 2007, Fedayns targeted Manchester United fans during a Champions League clash. The attacks resulted in 13 Red Devils fans hospitalised, two were kept over night for serious health checks.

While, the year beforehand, Middlesbrough fans were attacked. According to a BBC report from the time, a 39-year-old needed surgery for his injuries while a 16-year-old was also caught up in the violence.
