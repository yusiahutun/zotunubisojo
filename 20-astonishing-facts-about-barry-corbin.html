Early Life and Education

Barry Corbin was born on October 16, 1940, in Lamesa, Texas. He grew up in a ranching family and developed a passion for acting at a young age.

Before pursuing a career in acting, Corbin served in the United States Marine Corps. His time in the military greatly influenced his later roles in war-themed films.

Corbin gained widespread recognition for his role as Maurice Minnifield in the critically acclaimed TV series “Northern Exposure.” His portrayal of the quirky and eccentric ex-astronaut won him critical acclaim and a loyal fan base.

Versatile Actor

Throughout his career, Corbin showcased his versatility as an actor by effortlessly transitioning between comedy, drama, and even voice acting.

Collaborations with the Coen Brothers

Corbin has collaborated with the renowned Coen Brothers on multiple occasions, appearing in films like “No Country for Old Men,” “Raising Arizona,” and “The Big Lebowski.”
