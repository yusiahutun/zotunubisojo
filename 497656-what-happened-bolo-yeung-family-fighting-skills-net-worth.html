What happened to Bolo Yeung? Family, fighting skills, net worth

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

Bolo Yeung is a Chinese-born Hong Kong martial artist who used to compete as a bodybuilder. However, he is most famous for being a film actor. Yeung appeared in several dozen martial arts films during the 1970s and 2000. One of his most famous roles includes the film Enter the Dragon, where he appeared alongside Bruce Lee. He also starred alongside actor Jean-Claude Van Damme in the film Bloodsport.

Bolo Yeung is currently retired and leads a quiet life in Los Angeles. But what else is there to know about him besides his career? Here is more about his background, career, and personal life.

Early life

Bolo Yeung was born Yeung Sze on July 3, 1946, in Guangzhou, Canton, mainland China. Not much is known about his parents, but his father was reportedly a local businessman.

Yeung spent his childhood in Guangzhou, where he started learning martial arts as a child. He studied Kung Fu under several masters in the city. Additionally, he also learnt aerobics and started doing weight training. Over time, he started competing as a powerlifter, eventually becoming the national power-lifting champion of mainland China.

Bolo Yeung was not satisfied with his life in China. Therefore, in the 1960s, he decided to escape the mainland (by swimming) and headed to Hong Kong, hoping for new opportunities and a better life.

Career

When Bolo Yeung arrived in Hong Kong, he started teaching bodybuilding. He also competed in bodybuilding events, eventually becoming the bodybuilding champion of the Mr Hong Kong event, a title he held for about ten years. Over time, Yeung attracted the attention of the Shaw Brothers, a production company based in Hong Kong, which saw him enter the acting world.

While working with the Shaw Brothers, Yeung continued to improve his martial arts skills, which increased his acting profile. As a result, he was featured in several prominent films by the company, including:

  • The Heroic Ones
  • Angry Guest
  • The Deadly Duo
  • The Wandering Swordsman
  • The Oath of Death

In 1971, he left Shaw Brothers to pursue other opportunities. While filming a commercial for Winston cigarettes, Yeung crossed parts with Bruce Lee, who was also part of the commercial. The two became friends, so Lee offered Yeung a role in Enter the Dragon film. Yeung portrayed the character of Bolo (which is how he got his stage name), the main antagonist in the film.

It was a huge hit, which helped to increase his profile as an actor and martial arts fighter. After Enter the Dragon, Yeung appeared in several other films, mostly as an antagonist. He also appeared in several more films alongside Bruce Lee, such as Enter Three Dragons and Way of the Dragon 2. Then, in 1988, Yeung got a role in the film, Bloodsport, where he starred alongside Jean-Claude Van Damme.

During the film, the two developed a friendship, which saw Van Damme offer Yeung a role in another film Double Impact. After appearing in Van Damme's film, Yeung's acting profile increased even further. This saw him appear in several more high-profile films, such as:

  • Iron Heart
  • TC 200
  • Tiger Claws
  • Fearless Tiger

His acting career continued to rise until around 1997 when he took a break from the film scene. Yeung briefly returned to acting in 2007 when he appeared in the film Blizhniy Boy: The Ultimate Fighter. Since then, he has only appeared in a 2015 film, Diamon Cartel. However, he most recently landed a role in a completed but yet-to-be-released Turkish film, AFEREZ.

Wife and children

The actor is married. Unfortunately, he is a private person and hasn't revealed anything about his wife, including her name. However, the couple has three children – two sons and a daughter. One of his sons, David Yeung, is a bodybuilder and actor, while his second son is a businessman.

Net worth and earnings

What is Bolo Yeung’s net worth? The actor is estimated to be worth around $1.5 million. He made most of his wealth through his acting career, where he appeared in over 100 films. He has also earned some money through bodybuilding.

What happened to Bolo Yeung?

Where is Bolo Yeung now? The actor currently lives in Monterey Park, California. He has since retired from acting, martial arts, and bodybuilding and spends most of his time with his family. However, despite retiring, Yeung still trains regularly and can often be spotted at his local gym.
