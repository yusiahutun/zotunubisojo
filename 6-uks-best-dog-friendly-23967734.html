6 of the UK's best dog-friendly holiday accommodation where it's paw the merrier

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, March 12, 2024

Dogs are known for being man’s best friend, and our pet pooches have helped many of us get through the pandemic.

In the last 12 months, a record number of families have taken on a new four-legged friend – including mine, who adopted Romanian rescue Stan just before Christmas.

He is loving UK life with his Westie brother Ernie but when lockdown lifts, we’re looking forward to travelling a bit further than our local park.

Most pet-friendly accommodation will happily accept two pooches – but if your furry family is a bit bigger than that, it doesn’t have to mean relaxing breaks are off the menu.

Here are some suggestions for multi-pet holidays that are truly dog-friendly, rather than just dog tolerant.

Brechfa Forest Barns, Carmarthenshire

You can’t beat the views from these three mountain-top retreats, near Pencader. The rustic barns, which sleep two to six, all have secure gardens and miles of forest walks on the doorstep.

Guests can also make the most of a dog park and sensory garden called Scentral Bark. Here, you’ll find a splash pool, trampoline and sandpit plus a doggy vending machine where you can buy treats, with all proceeds going to a local dog charity.

Unlimited pets are welcome for £15 each per stay, and they’ll be able to hang out with resident beagles, Oscar and Alfie.

Weekend breaks start from £395.

brechfaforestbarns.co.uk

Letheringham Mill, Suffolk

For a luxurious home from home, check in to one of the four stylish cottages on the banks of the River Deben. From the moment you drive through the gate, you can unclip your dog’s lead and let them roam freely around the seven-acre site, which is surrounded by water.

The picture-postcard cottages, which sleep two to four, all come with a crate containing doggy essentials. Pets stay for free and there are no limits on numbers.

The resident Labradoodles, Teddy, Amber and Lola, also love keeping doggy guests entertained. Prices start from £120 per night (minimum stay of three nights). letheringhammill.co.uk

Developing Dogs, Cambs

If you have a reactive dog or one that could do with some extra training, check in to one of these three stylish cottages, near Ely. The Developing Dogs site is owned and run by Sian Ryan, from BBC2’s Me and My Dog, and her training centre – where you can book in for one-to-one sessions – is right next door.

All of the cottages, which sleep two to four, have secure gardens and hot tubs, and guests will also have pre-booked access to eight acres of securely fenced fields. There is no limit on the number of dogs and no extra charge for them. Short breaks start from £575, with discounts for returning guests. developingdogs.co.uk

Pawprints Holidays, Lincolnshire

If you’re looking for a quiet break by the sea, check out these three bungalows in Sutton-on-Sea and Hogsthorpe, which sleep four to six people.

All have secure gardens, wipe-clean flooring and are within easy walking distance of beautiful sandy beaches.

There is no limit on the number of dogs allowed at the properties – which are only rented to dog owners – and no extra charge, either. The most they’ve had to stay at once is 15!

Dog crates, steps, towels, bowls and poo bags can also be provided upon request. Week-long stays start from £650. pawprintsholidays.com

Balmaghie Dog-Friendly Holidays, Dumfries & Galloway

Relax in one of three cosy lodges, near Castle Douglas, which have 18 acres of fenced-in woodland to explore.

Dogs and their owners can have it all to themselves too, because you padlock the gate behind you when you enter.

The modern lodges, which sleep up to six, all have outdoor decking and secure gardens with 6ft fences.

A free dogsitting service is also available, upon request.

There is no limit on the number of dogs allowed on-site and no additional charge to bring pets along. Prices start from £70 per night. dogfriendlyholidaysscotland.co.uk

Boturnell Farm Cottages, Cornwall

If you’re looking for somewhere quirky to stay, head to Boturnell, in the heart of the Cornish countryside. It has everything from shepherd’s huts to Nissen huts, all of which have secure enclosed gardens.

There is access to eight acres of woodland and you’re just a short drive from the coast, too. Unlimited numbers of pets can stay for free – and they don’t have to be dogs, either. There are alpacas, goats and pigs onsite but cats, rabbits, parrots and tortoises are also welcome. You can even bring your horse for a small livery charge. Four-night breaks start from £200. dogs-holiday.co.uk
